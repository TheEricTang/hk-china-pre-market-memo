Morning Market Memo | 23 Jul 2026 | HK/China Pre-Open

(covers 22 Jul 2026 16:00 HKT close → 23 Jul 2026 07:40 HKT research cutoff)

  1. 01

    Overnight US markets – tech and ADRs softer: The Dow slipped 0.01%, S&P 500 fell 0.14% and Nasdaq lost 0.57%. Nvidia rose more than 2% and AMD gained over 1%, while Micron fell more than 1%; Super Micro jumped nearly 20% and Dell gained over 9%. The Nasdaq Golden Dragon index dropped 1.80%, with NetEase down more than 6%, Bilibili over 3% and Alibaba over 1%. WTI rose 2.54% to US$86.48/bbl, COMEX gold gained 1.44% to US$4,135/oz and silver rose 1.57% to US$60.04/oz, supporting the HK energy and precious-metals complexes. [Eastmoney] [AP]

  2. 02

    Alphabet and Tesla – mixed post-close results: Alphabet reported second-quarter revenue of US$119.8bn, above consensus, with Google Cloud also beating expectations; the company raised its 2026 capital-spending guidance to US$195bn–205bn, but shares fell about 4% after hours. Tesla dropped around 3% after hours as profit missed expectations despite stronger-than-forecast revenue, a negative near-term read-through for HK-listed EV makers. [Eastmoney] [Sharecast]

  3. 03

    China–US relations – Wang Yi met Rubio: Foreign Minister Wang Yi met US Secretary of State Marco Rubio in Manila and called on Washington to respect China’s core interests, uphold the one-China principle and manage differences. Wang said both sides should translate their leaders’ consensus into government-wide action, keeping the meeting relevant for China internet, exporters and other US-policy-sensitive names. [Caixin]

  4. 04

    Cross-border e-commerce – Beijing pushes back against European restrictions: MOFCOM said France’s “anti-ultra-fast-fashion” law discriminates against SHEIN, Temu and AliExpress and warned that China would respond if corporate rights were harmed. Separately, the ministry opposed the European Commission’s €550mn Digital Services Act fine on Alibaba’s AliExpress, calling it a discriminatory digital barrier. [MOFCOM] [Caixin]

  5. 05

    Zhejiang AI investment: Governor Liu Jie told a provincial government meeting to map the full AI supply chain, deepen national AI application pilot facilities and systematically prepare and implement major projects. The policy signal is relevant to Zhejiang-based computing, software, data-centre and automation companies. [Eastmoney]

  6. 06

    Beijing state capital – further equity purchases planned: Beijing State-owned Capital Operation and Management said it has deployed nearly RMB10bn of proprietary funds into the stock market and remains committed to long-term holdings in controlled listed companies. It plans further purchases through its own balance sheet, securities subsidiary and public-fund platform, including support for the Beijing Stock Exchange. [中新经纬]

  7. 07

    Zhongji Innolight – potential HK$55bn Hong Kong IPO: Zhongji Innolight (300308 CH/3308 HK) launched its Hong Kong offering of 54.5mn H-shares at a maximum HK$1,010 each, implying proceeds of up to roughly HK$55bn. Pricing is scheduled for 28 July and listing for 30 July; the transaction could become Hong Kong’s largest IPO this year and adds a major AI-optics name to the market. [Caixin] [华盛资讯]

  8. 08

    Local-government financing – bond reviews tighten: Shanghai and Shenzhen exchange reviews for local-government financing vehicles have reportedly added three new “red lines” on top of existing “335” criteria, slowing issuance by weaker district- and county-level platforms. Restricted capital-market access may shift more refinancing pressure to banks and accelerate consolidation among smaller LGFVs. [Caixin]

  9. 09

    Public funds – TMT concentration rises: Chinese public funds generated approximately RMB1.94tn of profit in the second quarter as the AI-computing chain rallied. TMT reached 62.35% of active equity-fund holdings, with Zhongji Innolight and Eoptolink the two largest positions; total industry assets reached about RMB38.6tn, highlighting both strong technology exposure and concentration risk. [Caixin] [Eastmoney]

  10. 10

    Wanhua Chemical – interim dividend proposed: Wanhua Chemical’s (600309 CH) chairman proposed a 2026 interim distribution equal to at least 25% of first-half attributable net profit, subject to the company’s formal approval process. The announcement reinforces the listed-company shareholder-return theme in the chemicals sector. [Eastmoney]

  11. 11

    Cancellation buybacks – storage and railway names: Baiwei Storage (688525 CH) proposed a RMB200mn–250mn buyback for cancellation, while Daqin Railway (601006 CH) proposed RMB400mn–500mn for the same purpose. Anfu Technology (603031 CH) separately announced a RMB100mn–150mn value-support buyback, extending the post-close cluster of shareholder-return measures. [Eastmoney]

  12. 12

    Hong Kong listing pipeline and control change: Orbbec (688322 CH) said it is preparing an H-share offering and Hong Kong listing, with terms still under discussion. Lianchuang Electronic (002036 CH), meanwhile, will suspend trading from today for up to two sessions while its controlling shareholder plans a transaction that may change corporate control. [Eastmoney]

  13. 13

    Grid and overseas infrastructure orders: XJ Electric (000400 CH) and subsidiaries won approximately RMB1.245bn of State Grid contracts covering converter valves, measurement devices, switchgear and secondary equipment. PowerChina (601669 CH) signed an approximately RMB8.925bn subcontract for the transmission system of an Iraqi desalination project, supporting the power-equipment and overseas engineering chains. [Eastmoney]

  14. 14

    AI and robotics capex: Aohai Technology (002993 CH) plans to invest RMB570mn in a Wuhan R&D and production base covering NEV control systems, AI-computing infrastructure and embodied-intelligence robotics. Pingzhi Information (300571 CH) was named first-ranked bidder for the RMB300mn Tianfu Intelligent Computing Centre Phase IV computing-services procurement. [Eastmoney]

  15. 15

    Dongshan Precision – overseas optical-module theft confirmed: Dongshan Precision (002384 CH) confirmed that optical modules were stolen from an overseas warehouse but said the loss was below disclosure thresholds and materially lower than the tens-of-millions-of-dollars figure circulating online. The clarification may contain the direct earnings concern, although it keeps operational controls in focus for the PCB and optics name. [36Kr]
