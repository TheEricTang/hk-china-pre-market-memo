Morning Market Memo | 24 Jul 2026 | HK/China Pre-Open

(covers 23 Jul 2026 16:00 HKT close → 24 Jul 2026 07:20 HKT research cutoff)

  1. 01

    **Capital markets – CSRC signals stronger stabilisation tools:** The CSRC’s post-close regulatory-work statement called for more precise counter-cyclical adjustment, a steady increase in the scale and proportion of long-term funds entering equities, and stronger preparations against global volatility and cross-border risk transmission. The message supports brokers, insurers and higher-quality listed companies after recent market swings. [CSRC] [Eastmoney]

  2. 02

    **PBOC – RMB500bn MLF operation today:** The central bank said it will conduct RMB500bn of one-year medium-term lending facility operations on 24 July through fixed-quantity, rate-tender and multiple-price allocation. The injection is intended to keep banking-system liquidity ample and is the main onshore funding event before the open. [Eastmoney]

  3. 03

    **ChangXin Memory – STAR Market listing set for Monday:** ChangXin Memory Technologies (688825 CH) will begin trading on 27 July at an IPO price of RMB8.66 per share. Post-offering share capital will be 66.88bn shares before exercise of the greenshoe, making the listing a major valuation marker for China’s DRAM, semiconductor-equipment and materials chains. [Eastmoney]

  4. 04

    **AI PCB capacity – Pengding and Red Board commit RMB15bn:** Pengding Holdings (002938 CH) approved a RMB10bn Shenzhen campus for high-end AI substrates and flexible circuit boards, with construction running from July 2026 to 2033. Red Board Technology (603459 CH) separately approved up to RMB5bn for high-end mSAP precision PCB capacity, adding another fresh catalyst for the AI-server and advanced-packaging supply chain. [Eastmoney] [Sina]

  5. 05

    **Indium phosphide – Yunnan Germanium wins large supply contract:** Yunnan Germanium (002428 CH) signed an agreement to supply InP wafers worth an estimated RMB570m–855m, equivalent to 53.5%–80.2% of its 2025 revenue, with deliveries scheduled from August 2026 through December 2027. Xingye Technology (002674 CH) also agreed to pay RMB55m for an InP substrate business, keeping optical communications and compound-semiconductor materials in focus. [Eastmoney] [Eastmoney]

  6. 06

    **Memory chips – Puran profit surge; Jiangbolong buyback:** Puran Semiconductor (688766 CH) expects first-half net profit of about RMB825m, up roughly 1,925%, citing higher memory-chip prices, improved supply structure and volume growth. Jiangbolong Electronics (301308 CH) said its chairman proposed a RMB400m–800m share repurchase for employee incentives, reinforcing the positive overnight read-through from US memory names. [NBD] [NBD]

  7. 07

    **China Shenhua – two new coal-power units enter operation:** China Shenhua (601088 CH/1088 HK) said the final 660MW units at its Dingzhou Phase III and Cangdong Phase III projects completed trial operation and entered commercial service. The completed projects are expected to supply about 11.9bn kWh of electricity and 20.14m gigajoules of heating annually, improving Beijing-Tianjin-Hebei power security. [Eastmoney]

  8. 08

    **Power storage and dividends:** China Southern Power Grid Energy Storage (600995 CH) reported first-half net profit of RMB1.17bn, up 40.3%, helped by stronger hydropower generation and new pumped-storage capacity. China Southern Power Grid Technology (688248 CH) separately proposed an interim dividend equal to at least 40% of first-half net profit, supporting the grid-equipment and energy-storage complex. [Eastmoney]

  9. 09

    **HK buybacks – SF Holding purchases H shares:** SF Holding (6936 HK/002352 CH) repurchased 436,800 H shares on Thursday at HK$32.62–33.24, spending HK$14.43m. The shares were placed into treasury, extending the logistics group’s broader capital-return programme across its A- and H-share listings. [Sina]

  10. 10

    **Oil services – Zhongman flags Iraq disruption:** Zhongman Petroleum (603619 CH) said nine of its 14 drilling teams deployed in Iraq remain suspended because of the regional conflict. The company expects first-half net profit of RMB88.7m–106m, down 64.7%–70.5%, tempering the benefit from the overnight surge in crude prices. [Eastmoney]

  11. 11

    **Lithium – broker sees firm summer production:** CSC Financial said maintenance at some lithium-salt plants is reducing supply while battery-material production remains firm, with July output expected to continue growing and August scheduling also positive. The call supports lithium miners and converters after domestic lithium carbonate futures gained more than 2% Thursday. [Eastmoney]

  12. 12

    **US trade policy – new Section 301 tariffs on 60 economies:** The US Trade Representative imposed additional tariffs linked to enforcement of forced-labour import bans, generally setting rates at 10% or 12.5% and covering 60 economies. The measures take effect on 24 July US time and have potential supply-chain implications for Asian electronics, textiles and exporters. [USTR]

  13. 13

    **Intel – earnings beat lifts shares after hours:** Intel reported second-quarter revenue of US$16.13bn, up 25% and above the US$14.43bn consensus, while adjusted EPS of US$0.42 doubled expectations. Data-centre and AI revenue reached US$6.26bn; stronger third-quarter guidance sent the shares up as much as 13% after hours, a constructive read-through for semiconductor equipment despite the weak regular session. [Eastmoney]

  14. 14

    **US close – technology and EV shares lead declines:** The Dow fell 0.97% to 51,711.65, the S&P 500 lost 1.21% to 7,408.30 and the Nasdaq dropped 2.15% to 25,137.69. Tesla sank 14.52%, Alphabet fell more than 7% and Nvidia, TSMC and Broadcom lost over 1%; memory outperformed, with Micron up more than 3% and SK Hynix above 2%. No verified Golden Dragon close was available in the retained wrap by cutoff, so an aggregate China-ADR figure is omitted rather than estimated. [Eastmoney]

  15. 15

    **Cross-assets – oil jumps while precious metals retreat:** WTI settled 6.37% higher at US$92.36 a barrel as Middle East hostilities intensified; Brent briefly touched US$100. Spot gold fell 1.95% to US$4,049.56 and silver lost 3.47% to US$57.66, while the DXY rose 0.32% to 101.445 and the US 10-year yield climbed to 4.697%. [Eastmoney]
