Morning Market Memo | 23 Jul 2026 | HK/China Pre-Open

(covers 22 Jul 2026 16:00 HKT close → 23 Jul 2026 07:55 HKT research cutoff)

  1. 01

    **Overnight US markets:** The Dow slipped 0.01%, the S&P 500 fell 0.14% and the Nasdaq lost 0.57%. Nvidia rose more than 2% and AMD gained over 1%, while Micron fell more than 1%; China ADRs were weaker, including Alibaba -1.2%, PDD -1.5%, NIO -2.3% and XPeng -3.6%, although Nasdaq’s public page had not posted the July 22 Golden Dragon close by cutoff. Spot gold gained 1.28% to US$4,130/oz, WTI rose 2.54% to US$86.48, DXY eased 0.05% to 101.125 and the US 10-year yield reached 4.663%. [Eastmoney]

  2. 02

    **Oil/Hormuz – supply threat escalates:** Brent gained 3.4% to US$94.07 and briefly traded above US$95 as tanker traffic through the Strait of Hormuz remained constrained. Iran’s military said the strait remained closed except through designated procedures and threatened regional energy infrastructure if the US attacks Iranian infrastructure, keeping oil producers and refiners in focus. [AP] [Eastmoney]

  3. 03

    **Alphabet – strong growth, heavier AI spending:** Second-quarter revenue rose 24% to US$119.8bn, beating expectations, with Google Cloud posting its strongest growth in several quarters. Alphabet raised 2026 capital-spending guidance to US$195bn–205bn and fell more than 3% after hours, but the spending plan supports demand for AI servers, optics, memory and power equipment. [Eastmoney] [AP]

  4. 04

    **Tesla – revenue beat, margins missed:** Second-quarter revenue increased 26% to US$28.24bn, above expectations, but adjusted EPS of US$0.33 missed the US$0.51 consensus and free cash flow turned negative at US$1.09bn. Tesla said Fremont production of the Optimus humanoid robot should begin later this year; the print is relevant to the China EV, autonomous-driving and robotics chains. [Eastmoney]

  5. 05

    **Central SOEs – reform and technology priorities:** SASAC’s July 21–22 central-enterprise meeting set six second-half priorities, including original innovation, core-technology breakthroughs, industrial upgrading and implementation of a new SOE reform programme. Central SOEs generated RMB1.4tn of first-half profit, while fixed-asset investment rose 4.5%; SASAC also called for an AI-for-scientific-research plan. [Eastmoney]

  6. 06

    **Sports industry – first national “sports power” plan:** The State Council-approved 15th Five-Year sports plan targets an industry exceeding RMB7tn by 2030, with per-capita sports space around four square metres and regular exercise participation near 40%. The plan covers facilities, outdoor sports, technology and training bases, relevant to sportswear, venues, tourism and fitness operators. [Eastmoney]

  7. 07

    **Beijing state capital – nearly RMB10bn deployed into equities:** Beijing State-owned Capital Operation and Management said it had committed almost RMB10bn of proprietary funds to the stock market and would remain a long-term holder of controlled listed-company stakes. The statement adds a fresh state-capital support signal for Beijing SOEs and technology holdings. [Eastmoney]

  8. 08

    **China–US – Wang Yi met Marco Rubio:** Foreign Minister Wang Yi met the US secretary of state in Manila and urged Washington to respect China’s core interests, uphold the one-China principle and manage disputes. Wang also described 2026 as an important year for bilateral relations following the two presidents’ Beijing meeting, relevant to exporters and tariff-sensitive technology names. [Eastmoney]

  9. 09

    **Jerry – US$1.465bn data-centre power order:** Jerry’s controlled subsidiary accepted a gas-turbine generator order worth US$1.465bn, or about RMB9.95bn, from an international cloud provider. The order equals 61.33% of Jerry’s 2025 revenue and is scheduled for phased delivery by November 2027, highlighting data-centre demand for off-grid power equipment. [Eastmoney]

  10. 10

    **Baiwei Storage – cancellation buyback proposed:** Baiwei’s chairman proposed a RMB200m–250m share repurchase, with all purchased shares to be cancelled and registered capital reduced; the proposed ceiling is RMB558.44 per share. Daqin Railway separately proposed a RMB400m–500m cancellation buyback, while Enjie announced a RMB100m–200m employee-incentive repurchase. [Eastmoney]

  11. 11

    **Jin’an Guoji – RMB2bn copper-clad-laminate expansion:** The company signed an investment agreement with Zhuhai’s Jinwan district for capacity of 20m copper-clad-laminate sheets and 40m metres of prepreg annually. The project is a fresh capacity catalyst for PCB materials and the AI-server electronics supply chain. [Eastmoney]

  12. 12

    **Yisheng – first-half profit jumped:** First-half revenue rose 28.44% to RMB1.697bn and attributable profit reached RMB308m, up 4,897.29%; the company proposed a RMB0.15-per-share cash dividend. Second-quarter profit was approximately RMB204m, nearly double the first quarter, supporting the poultry-breeding read-through. [Eastmoney]

  13. 13

    **Lianxun Instruments – semiconductor-testing profit surge:** The company expects first-half attributable profit of RMB510m–580m, up 802%–926%. Implied second-quarter profit is RMB391m–461m, representing sequential growth of 228%–287%, a fresh catalyst for domestic semiconductor testing and equipment names. [Eastmoney]

  14. 14

    **Innovent – weekly oral GLP-1 filed for clinical testing:** Innovent disclosed a clinical-trial application for what it describes as the world’s first oral, once-weekly small-molecule GLP-1 candidate. The filing extends the company’s obesity and diabetes pipeline and is relevant to the broader Hong Kong-listed biotech complex. [Eastmoney]

  15. 15

    **Dobot – A-share IPO cleared:** Hong Kong-listed robotics company Dobot passed its mainland IPO review, with regulators questioning the prudence of its forecast to reach profitability in 2028 and the adequacy of risk disclosure. The approval provides a fresh H-to-A catalyst for humanoid and industrial-robotics names. [Eastmoney]

  16. 16

    **Cathay Pacific and Unisound – positive profit alerts:** Both Hong Kong-listed companies issued positive profit alerts after the close, while dairy producer Baifei resubmitted its Hong Kong listing application. Cathay is the direct aviation read-through; Unisound adds an AI-software earnings catalyst. [Eastmoney]

  17. 17

    **Dongshan Precision – overseas theft confirmed but impact contained:** The company confirmed that optical modules were stolen from an overseas warehouse, but said the value was well below online claims of tens of millions of US dollars and the profit impact did not meet its disclosure threshold. The clarification should temper the immediate read-through for Dongshan and the optical-component chain. [Eastmoney]
