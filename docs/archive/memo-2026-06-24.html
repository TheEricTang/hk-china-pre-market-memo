Morning Market Memo | 24 Jun 2026 | HK/China Pre-Open

- Global chip selloff, Korea rebounds: US tech fell hard Tuesday — Nasdaq −2.21%, S&P 500 −1.43% and the Philadelphia Semiconductor Index −7.87%, with Micron down over 13% and Nvidia −4.15%. Asian chips are bouncing Wednesday: the KOSPI rebounded about 4% after a report that Samsung will buy back ₩90tn of shares over three years. Sets a volatile tone for HK/China semiconductors such as SMIC (981 HK) and Hua Hong (1347 HK). [[Eastmoney](https://finance.eastmoney.com/a/202606243780173196.html)] [[Yonhap](https://finance.yahoo.com/markets/stocks/articles/samsung-electronics-plans-90-trln-013557501.html)]

  1. 01

    Iran inspections disputed; Hormuz reopening: The US and Iran gave conflicting accounts — Trump said Iran "completely agreed" to UN nuclear inspections, while Tehran's foreign ministry denied it, so treat this as unresolved. A 14-point framework covers reopening the Strait of Hormuz, where transits are rising, though Iran says it will keep control. Softer oil supports HK/China airlines and refiners; watch CNOOC and PetroChina. [CBS] [CNN]

  2. 02

    SSE "Quality & Returns 2.0": The Shanghai Stock Exchange issued a public initiative on 23 June urging all Shanghai-listed firms to set quantified targets — ROE, gross margin, output/sales, R&D outcomes or self-chosen KPIs — backed by concrete measures, plus higher and more frequent dividends and buyback-and-cancellation. It also tells firms to avoid hot-concept speculation and improve disclosure, and comes with a template of model indicators. Supports the listed-company quality and shareholder-return theme. [Eastmoney]

  3. 03

    Liu Guozhong on biopharma, brain-computer interface: Vice Premier Liu Guozhong, inspecting Jiangsu on 22–23 June, called for building biopharma into a new pillar industry and cultivating brain-computer interface as a future industry, with stronger basic research and faster high-level sci-tech self-reliance. He visited biopharma, AI and cloud firms and Nanjing University's brain-computer interface institute. Positive for biopharma and BCI-related names. [Xinhua]

  4. 04

    SOE reform push: At a national video conference on 23 June, Vice Premier Zhang Guoqing urged deeper state-asset and SOE reform — focus on core businesses, curb unrelated diversification, multi-layer structures and excess leverage, advance restructuring under the "three concentrations," and adopt value-creation-oriented, classified performance assessment including SOE value-added accounting. Supports the central-SOE reform and "value creation" theme. [news.cn]

  5. 05

    Foreign-investment "15 measures": MOFCOM, the NDRC and the Ministry of Finance issued an action plan on 22 June with 15 measures to stabilise foreign investment, including support for qualified key foreign firms to list in mainland China, wider services-sector opening, foreign use of risk tools such as treasury-bond futures, and an upgrade of CEPA with Hong Kong and Macao. Jan–May actual FDI was −8.6% YoY, a narrower decline than earlier. Supportive for financial-opening and HK/Macao services linkages. [sina]

  6. 06

    "618" growth slows sharply: Star Map data put 2026 "618" total online GMV at RMB934bn, up just 4% YoY versus 20.9% a year ago. Integrated platforms (Taobao/Tmall, JD, Pinduoduo, Douyin, Kuaishou) rose only 0.9% to RMB863.6bn, instant retail jumped 112.3% to RMB62.8bn, and community group-buying fell 39.6% to RMB7.6bn. A soft signal for e-commerce and consumption names, with instant retail the clear bright spot. [jrj]

  7. 07

    Domestic supercomputer tops TOP500: At ISC 2026 in Hamburg, China's fully home-grown "Lingsheng" supercomputer led the global TOP500 with 2.19 EFlops of sustained double-precision performance — China's first number-one in nine years and the first system sustaining over 2 EFlops, built by the National Supercomputing Center in Shenzhen. Positive for the domestic computing-power and chip self-sufficiency theme. [sina]

  8. 08

    Shenzhen "six networks" plan: Shenzhen's Party Committee met on 23 June to push its "six networks" build-out — water, a stronger/greener/smarter power grid, a computing-power network (capacity, interconnection and power-compute coordination), next-gen communications (national backbone interconnects, 6G, "dual-10-gigabit" and satellite applications), underground pipe networks and logistics. Supports power-grid, computing-power, 6G and satellite names. [Eastmoney]

  9. 09

    MIIT NEV "15th Five-Year" plan: At a State Council Information Office briefing on 23 June, MIIT official Guo Shougang said the ministry will accelerate a "15th Five-Year" plan for the intelligent-connected NEV industry and push next-generation batteries, automotive chips, operating systems and autonomous driving, alongside trade-in support and orderly competition. Supportive for the NEV and auto-chip supply chain. [Eastmoney]

  10. 10

    Same-day car purchase and registration: MIIT and the Ministry of Public Security issued a notice (23 June) enabling real-time sharing of vehicle-certificate data so domestic passenger cars can be bought, taxed and registered the same day, benefiting over 20m vehicles a year. A modest boost for auto consumption. [Eastmoney]

  11. 11

    NIO reaches 9,000 power stations: NIO (9866 HK) brought its 9,000th charging/battery-swap station online in Ankang, Shaanxi on 23 June, with more launched the same day in Hefei, Tangshan, Wuxi and Wuhan; it now has 9,003 stations and over 200m cumulative services, and plans 1,000+ new swap stations in 2026. Reinforces NIO's battery-swap network advantage. [Eastmoney]

  12. 12

    Finance for women's employment: The PBOC, the National Financial Regulatory Administration and the All-China Women's Federation issued guidance to expand financial support for women's employment and entrepreneurship — easier guarantee loans (lower or waived counter-guarantees for good-credit borrowers), debt-financing and supply-chain tools, and a "training + credit" model. A social-policy measure with knock-on support for consumption and training finance. [Eastmoney]

  13. 13

    Hong Kong IPO market stays hot: 10 companies are due to list in Hong Kong this week, and Wind data point to about 83 IPOs in 1H 2026 raising over HK$200bn — well above a year earlier, with full-year proceeds expected among the world's top three. Positive for HKEX (388 HK) and the brokerage/underwriting chain. [STCN]

  14. 14

    Alibaba sues US Defense Department: Alibaba (9988 HK) filed suit on 23 June in a San Jose federal court seeking removal from the Pentagon's "Chinese military companies" list, which was expanded to 188 entities on 8 June; it called the designation a due-process and free-speech violation and said its board has no military ties. Headline risk on the US-China/listing front for Alibaba and other named Chinese tech firms. [Bloomberg]

  15. 15

    Cathay Pacific May traffic: Cathay Pacific (293 HK) carried 17% more passengers in May YoY with ASKs up 10%, and first-five-month passengers up 19%, helped by connecting traffic via Hong Kong (network load factor 87%). With HK Express, the group flew over 3.3m passengers (+14%) and Cathay Cargo over 150,000 tonnes (+11%). Constructive for Cathay and HK aviation/travel names. [Cathay Pacific]
