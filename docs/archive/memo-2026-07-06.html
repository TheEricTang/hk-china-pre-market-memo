Morning Market Memo | 06 Jul 2026 | HK/China Pre-Open

(covers 03 Jul 16:00 HKT close → 06 Jul 9:30 HKT open)

  1. 01

    A-share trading rules go live today: the revised SSE/SZSE/BSE trading rules take effect this session. After-hours fixed-price trading (15:05–15:30) is extended from STAR/ChiNext to all A-shares and ETFs; main-board ST/*ST daily price limits widen from 5% to 10% (150+ stocks affected); ChiNext introduces market makers and moves block-trade confirmation to intraday; fund closing prices shift to a closing auction. First-day mechanics may change close and after-hours liquidity patterns today. [STCN] [STCN]

  2. 02

    PBOC liquidity injection today: the central bank announced Friday evening it will conduct a RMB1,000bn 3-month (91-day) outright reverse repo operation on 6 July — per Pengpai, the first month of net injection via the tool after four consecutive months of net withdrawal. A supportive funding signal for banks, brokers and rate-sensitive A/H names into the new week. [Eastmoney]

  3. 03

    CSRC refinancing overhaul: the CSRC opened consultation Friday on revised refinancing rules — a shelf-registration system for competitive private placements (one registration, multiple issues, 2-year validity), a unified market-price pricing mechanism, and simplified conditions for placements to controlling shareholders. The small-fast refinancing cap doubles from RMB300m to RMB600m (RMB1bn for issuers with net assets above RMB100bn; BSE cap RMB100m→200m). Positive for brokers' investment-banking pipelines and frequent A-share issuers. [STCN] [Eastmoney]

  4. 04

    Memory-chip capex and price wave (weekend): Micron formally began its ¥1.5tn (~US$9.3bn) Hiroshima fab expansion on Saturday for HBM and advanced memory (shipments from ~summer 2028); Samsung plans two new memory fabs in Gwangju and SK Hynix two in South Jeolla, combined ~₩800tn (~US$522bn). Samsung has verbally notified customers of a ~20% QoQ rise in Q3 DRAM average selling prices, confirmed by two industry sources; TrendForce sees Q3 conventional DRAM contract prices +13–18% QoQ and NAND +10–15%, while UBS raised its Q3 DDR forecast to +32% QoQ (from +17%) and NAND to +30%. Fresh catalyst for the HK/A storage, module and semi-equipment complex at the open. [STCN] [Eastmoney]

  5. 05

    H1 profit alerts cluster (Fri and Sun evenings): storage-module maker Jiangbolong guided H1 net profit up 62,204%–74,394%; Dongfang Shenghong guided RMB4.2–5.0bn, up 987%–1,195% (Sunday); Yongding guided RMB500–700m, up 57%–120% on optical-fiber volume and price gains (Sunday); Hangdian and Dongyue Silicon each guided >900% growth (Friday). Confirms earnings momentum in storage, optical comms and select chemicals into results season. [STCN]

  6. 06

    NEV/energy-saving vehicle tax change: MoF, the State Taxation Administration and MIIT announced Friday evening that from 1 Jan 2027 the half-rate vehicle-and-vessel tax for energy-saving vehicles and the exemption for pure-electric, plug-in hybrid and fuel-cell commercial vehicles will be cancelled; pure-electric and fuel-cell passenger cars remain outside the tax's scope. A cost consideration for commercial-vehicle and hybrid makers rather than EV passenger-car names. [Eastmoney] [STCN]

  7. 07

    Retail fuel price cut, largest this year: from 24:00 Friday, domestic gasoline and diesel prices were cut RMB950/t and RMB915/t respectively (92# gasoline −RMB0.75/L). Positive for airlines and logistics operators; a negative marker for refiners. [Eastmoney]

  8. 08

    "Beautiful China" 15th Five-Year plan: the State Council issued the plan (Friday evening, per CLS via Eastmoney's digest) — steadily advance carbon peaking, reasonably control coal-power capacity and generation, lift the renewable share of power consumption with new demand covered by incremental clean energy, and build CCUS demonstration projects. Constructive for grid, renewables and nuclear names; a watch item for coal-power operators. [Eastmoney]

  9. 09

    Lithium chain: Tianci Materials (002709 CH) said Friday evening it terminated its 243kt lithium and fluorine new-materials project (planned investment RMB2.65bn), citing electrolyte overcapacity and persistent price declines. Separately, SMM reported battery-grade lithium carbonate spot rising Friday with sellers holding for above RMB170k/t. Supply discipline in electrolytes against firming lithium prices frames the battery-materials open. [STCN] [Eastmoney]

  10. 10

    Cell and gene therapy support: the NMPA published a consultation draft Friday (3 Jul) to optimize CGT review and approval — eligible products enter the 30-day IND review channel, with global synchronous R&D and international multi-center trials encouraged, focused on oncology, rare and genetic diseases. Supportive for HK/A innovative-drug and CXO names. [STCN]

  11. 11

    Capital-markets calendar: the CSRC approved IPO registrations for Makeng Mining (SSE main board) and Zhongsu New Materials (ChiNext) in Friday's batch. Two subscriptions this week — Longxin Intelligent (920117) today and Tynomab Friday. Roughly RMB101.6bn of lockup expiries hit this week across 40 companies, led by Yitang (RMB65.4bn). [STCN]

  12. 12

    Robotics/embodied-AI froth check: after the Wind embodied-intelligence index gained ~10% over four sessions, several A-share robotics-concept names issued clarification or risk-warning announcements over the weekend distancing themselves from the theme. Watch the HK/A robotics complex for follow-through versus cooling today. [STCN]

  13. 13

    Huawei "Tao's Law" V2: Huawei semiconductor chief He Tingbo published version 2 of her time-scaling theory paper (《面向多层级电子系统的时间缩微理论》, dubbed "Tao's Law") on ChinaXiv on 3 July, adding engineering implementation detail, measured data and a product roadmap to the post-Moore scaling framework first released in May. Sentiment driver for the domestic semiconductor complex. [Eastmoney]

  14. 14

    Guotai Haitong (601211 CH) H1 alert: the broker guides H1 net profit of RMB20.0–20.5bn, with ex-items profit up 164%–171% YoY — a record half, and a record Q2 (ex-items +137%–146% QoQ). First major broker print of the season, landing alongside the CSRC refinancing easing; positive read-through for HK-listed Chinese brokers. [Eastmoney]

  15. 15

    Optical-module chain: Pengding Holdings (002938 CH) launched a private placement of up to RMB9.6bn to fund an AI-server and high-speed optical-module HDI board project. Separately, Zhongji Innolight (300308 CH) said market talk that a key upstream material had been "blockaded" is untrue, citing normal procurement channels. [Eastmoney]

  16. 16

    Gold — sell-side turns constructive: JPMorgan sees gold range-bound near term then recovering, averaging US$4,300/oz in Q3 2026 and US$4,500 in Q4, with further upside in 2027; HSBC says US Treasury yields are now the main driver of gold and flags its weaker equity-hedge performance this year. COMEX gold rose 1.49% to US$4,187.3 in Friday's abbreviated session, silver +2.87% to US$62.815. [Eastmoney]

  17. 17

    Iran and oil watch: mourning and funeral rites for Ali Khamenei run 4–9 July, with the Tehran procession today and stages in Qom, Najaf/Karbala and Mashhad to follow; ~100 countries sent official delegations. Trump remarked publicly on the funeral that a single strike "could take out Iran's entire leadership, but we won't do that — then there'd be no one to negotiate with." A headline-risk window for crude as Western energy futures reopen tonight after the long weekend. [NBD]

  18. 18

    Overnight US markets and cross-assets (data): US stock and bond markets were closed Friday for Independence Day (4 July fell on Saturday) — no overnight cash session; US trading resumes tonight HKT, with chips/storage having fallen two straight sessions into Thursday's close on AI-trade repricing, the backdrop against which today's memory headlines land. In Friday's abbreviated Western sessions: COMEX gold +1.49% to US$4,187.3, silver +2.87% to US$62.815; LME copper +US$40 to US$13,366/t, tin +US$1,668 to US$52,628/t; DXY eased to 100.845; onshore CNY closed 6.7804, up 62 pips; overnight Shibor fell 3.7bp to 1.369%. WTI last settled Thursday at US$68.78 (+0.13%). Ahead this week: the Fed's June meeting minutes, and China June CPI/PPI on Thursday 9 July. [Sina] [Eastmoney]
