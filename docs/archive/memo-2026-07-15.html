Morning Market Memo | 15 Jul 2026 | HK/China Pre-Open

(covers 14 Jul 16:00 HKT close → 15 Jul 9:30 HKT open)

  1. 01

    Overnight US – soft CPI lifts stocks, storage screams: US indices closed higher on the Tuesday session — Dow +0.02% (52,508.27), S&P 500 +0.38% (7,543.59), Nasdaq +0.9% (26,107.01). Memory/storage led hard: SK Hynix's US-listed line jumped 27% (closing 51% above its Korea shares), SanDisk +5%, Micron +5%; Nvidia and Intel each +4%, ASML and AMD +2%, while Qualcomm -3% and Arm -6%. Constructive lead for the HK/A semiconductor and storage complex. [Eastmoney]

  2. 02

    US June CPI turns negative – Fed hike bets slashed: headline CPI rose 3.5% YoY (est 3.8%, prior 4.2%) but fell 0.4% MoM — the first negative monthly print in six years (est -0.1%, prior +0.5%); core CPI +2.6% YoY. Short-rate futures jumped and traders sharply cut bets on a hike at the 28-29 Jul FOMC; the 10y yield fell ~5bp to 4.57% and the 2y ~10bp to 4.18%. Supportive risk backdrop into the HK open. [Eastmoney]

  3. 03

    IBM -25%, worst day since 1987 – "clients bought memory, not software": IBM flagged a Q2 revenue miss (prelim ~US$17.2bn, +1%; infrastructure -7%, software +5%, consulting flat), wiping out roughly US$70bn of market cap. CEO Krishna said clients redirected late-June capex into servers, storage and memory ahead of expected price hikes — a striking real-world signal reinforcing the memory up-cycle that is driving SK Hynix, Micron and A-share storage names. [Sina]

  4. 04

    PBOC RMB1.4tn 6-month buy-back reverse repo (today): to keep banking-system liquidity ample, the PBOC will conduct a RMB1,400bn outright (买断式) reverse repo on 15 Jul — 184-day tenor, maturing 15 Jan 2027 — via fixed-quantity, rate-tender, multiple-price bidding. Liquidity-supportive for onshore. [Eastmoney]

  5. 05

    Changxin (CXMT) IPO – 2026's largest A-share float: DRAM maker Changxin Technology (688825.SH) set its price at RMB8.66/share with subscription on 16 Jul, raising ~RMB29.5bn — the biggest A-share IPO of 2026 and the second-largest STAR Market listing ever, with strategic placement around 50%. As China's largest DRAM designer-manufacturer, it is a fresh catalyst for the storage complex (watch GigaDevice 兆易创新) and a near-term liquidity draw. [STCN]

  6. 06

    Storage earnings confirm the up-cycle: 德明利 (001309.SZ) guided H1 net profit to RMB5.7-6.5bn, swinging to profit, citing AI-driven demand and rising storage prices amid tight supply (though Q2 is seen down 5-29% q/q). Adds hard-numbers support to the memory-price momentum. [Eastmoney]

  7. 07

    Advanced-packaging & optics earnings surge: evening H1 preannouncements beat broadly across the AI-hardware chain — 通富微电 (002156.SZ) +288-337%, 长电科技 (600584.SH) +63-102%, 长飞光纤 (601869.SH) +711-914%, 东山精密 (002384.SZ) +283-296%, 光迅科技 (002281.SZ) +50-65%, 中天科技 (600522.SH) +50-60%. Supportive for the packaging/optical-module cluster. [Eastmoney]

  8. 08

    Insurers – large H1 beats: 中国人寿 (601628.SH) guided H1 net profit to roughly RMB128.9-137.1bn, up 215-235% YoY on investment gains. Positive read for the insurance and brokerage complex. [Eastmoney]

  9. 09

    Lithium and materials turn: 天齐锂业 (002466.SZ) guided H1 net profit to RMB2.85-4.25bn, up 3,276-4,935% YoY off a low base, and 中国巨石 (600176.SH) guided +65-85%. Watch lithium and glass-fibre/materials names. [Eastmoney]

  10. 10

    Commercial space – reusable-rocket policy: SASTIND's news centre said the third batch of guidelines for reusable-launch-vehicle R&D projects has been formally issued (14 Jul), with enterprises and institutions now able to apply. A forward catalyst for the commercial-space and satellite-application chain. [Eastmoney]

  11. 11

    Oil/geopolitics – Hormuz risk reignites: WTI settled +2.16% at US$79.83 after US CENTCOM said it resumed a naval blockade of Iran's ports and coast (effective ~04:00 HKT), with 20+ US warships in theatre; separately Trump said he will scrap the proposed 20% Hormuz cargo fee in favour of Gulf trade-and-investment deals. Relevant for oil, energy and shipping names. [Eastmoney]

  12. 12

    Gold back above $4,000: COMEX gold rose 1.31% to US$4,058.3/oz and silver +1.84% to US$59.04 as the soft CPI and a weaker dollar (DXY -0.31% to 100.92) boosted metals; LME copper +US$102 to 13,643 and tin +US$1,215 to 53,813. Positive for gold miners and non-ferrous. [Eastmoney]

  13. 13

    NY halts big data centres: New York became the first US state to impose a one-year moratorium on new data centres drawing 50MW or more. A demand-side flag for the AI-power/data-centre read-through, though China's build-out is domestically driven. [Eastmoney]

  14. 14

    China ADRs mixed: the Nasdaq Golden Dragon index slipped 0.11%; XPeng +3%, iQiyi and Li Auto +2%, NIO +1%, but Baidu -3% and NetEase -2%. A muted lead for HK's ADR-sensitive internet and EV names. [Eastmoney]

  15. 15

    Apple – rare downgrade plus on-device AI: KeyBanc cut Apple to Underweight, calling its valuation relative to the market unjustified on demand and growth concerns; separately Apple is in talks with startup PrismML over technology that compresses AI models to run on-device (up to 15x less memory). Watch Apple supply-chain names (Luxshare, AAC). [Eastmoney]
