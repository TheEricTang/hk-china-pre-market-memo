Morning Market Memo | 05 Jul 2026 | HK/China Pre-Open

(covers 03 Jul 16:00 HKT close → 05 Jul 9:30 HKT; today is Sunday — no HK session; items below feed Monday 06 Jul's open)

  1. 01

    PBOC restarts net injection – RMB1tn outright reverse repo Monday: The PBOC announced Friday evening it will conduct RMB1,000bn of 3-month (91-day) outright reverse repo on Monday 6 Jul against RMB800bn maturing this month — a net RMB200bn add that ends several months of shrinking rollovers. July has RMB1.7tn of outright reverse repos plus RMB400bn of MLF maturing; DR001/DR007 have risen back to the ~1.4% policy rate, and analysts (CITIC's Ming Ming, Golden Credit's Wang Qing) read the move as a return to supportive net injection of medium-term liquidity. Positive for banks/brokers and funding-sensitive names at Monday's open. [Sina/Yicai]

  2. 02

    A-share trading rules go live Monday: The revised SSE/SZSE/BSE trading rules take effect Mon 6 Jul — main-board ST/*ST daily price limits widen from 5% to 10%; after-hours fixed-price trading extends to all A-shares and ETFs; the SSE moves fund closing prices to a closing auction; the SZSE introduces market makers on ChiNext and widens the GEM block-trade confirmation window to trading hours (9:30–11:30, 13:00–15:30). Watch ST-name volatility and GEM liquidity from the open. [Sina/STCN]

  3. 03

    Longsys blowout preannouncement – storage supercycle catalyst: Longsys (301308 CH) said Friday after the close it expects H1 net profit of RMB9.2–11.0bn, up 62,204%–74,394% y/y, citing tight global memory-wafer supply against surging demand and renewed long-term wafer supply agreements; implied Q2 profit is RMB5.34–7.14bn, +38%–85% q/q on Q1's RMB3.86bn. Nothing is priced since the announcement landed post-close ahead of a three-day gap — fresh fuel for the HK/A storage and AI-hardware complex Monday. [Sina/券商中国]

  4. 04

    More Friday-evening H1 preannouncements: China Electronics Port (001287 CH) guided +176%–193% (RMB500–530m) on AI/data-centre demand and rising memory prices; Hangzhou Cable (603618 CH) +852%–958% (RMB360–400m) on fibre-optic volume and price recovery; Dongyue Silicone (300821 CH) +905%–952% on silicone price rises; Zhongjin Lingnan (000060 CH) +88%–115% on higher metal prices. Read-through to optical-fibre, silicone and base-metals peers in HK/A. [Sina/券商中国]

  5. 05

    Overseas-listing (HK IPO) pipeline – CSRC feedback batch: The CSRC's weekly filing-feedback disclosure (29 Jun–3 Jul), out Friday evening, put supplementary questions to Aqara/Lumi United (smart-home/AI — red-chip structure, weighted voting rights, no-controller determination, GenAI compliance under the 生成式AI管理办法), Sino Medical (688108 CH; share transfers and its brain–computer interface technology), Dier Laser (300776 CH) and Ganzhicao. The overseas/HK listing pipeline keeps building — relevant for HK brokers and the IPO calendar. [Sina]

  6. 06

    Hormuz / oil: The UK said Saturday (joint No.10 statement) that Oman has agreed to work with Britain and France to secure navigation in its sovereign waters, with the UK and France ready to deploy a broader multinational mission to support freedom of navigation through the Strait of Hormuz — another step in supply normalisation after the US–Iran MoU, with Doha technical talks reporting "positive progress." WTI settled Thursday at $67.79 (−0.03%), Brent $70.81 (−0.15%); NDRC cut domestic retail fuel prices by the year's largest margin (gasoline −RMB950/t, diesel −RMB915/t) effective Saturday. Softer crude backdrop for airlines/logistics, a headwind for oil producers at Monday's open. [Sina] [Sina/界面]

  7. 07

    Gold – JPMorgan call: JPMorgan said Saturday gold is likely rangebound near-term as key demand pockets weaken and the metal turns sensitive to real rates again, but kept a medium-term bullish view — Q3 2026 average ~$4,300/oz rising to ~$4,500 in Q4, with 2027 gains driven by continued central-bank buying and physical demand. Frames Monday's open for HK/A gold miners after the recent pullback. [Sina]

  8. 08

    Overnight US markets (mandatory data; note: US closed Friday): US markets were shut Friday 3 Jul for Independence Day (observed) and reopen Monday, so there was no Friday US or China-ADR session. The last close (Thu): Dow +1.14% to a record 52,899.42; Nasdaq −0.80% to 25,832.67; S&P 500 +0.01% at 7,483.24, after June payrolls of +57k vs +115k expected (unemployment 4.2%) pushed the 2-year yield lower and firmed bets the Fed pauses further hikes. Semis were the drag — SMH −5.2%, Teradyne and KLA −13%+, Micron −5%, Nvidia −1.4% — a soft setup for HK semis/AI hardware that Monday's Longsys guidance partially offsets; on the week the Dow gained 1.97%, Nasdaq 2.12%, S&P 1.76%. [Sina]

  9. 09

    Europe – record close Friday: With the US shut, the Stoxx Europe 600 rose 0.7% Friday to a record, a fourth straight weekly gain, led by tech with ASML the biggest contributor; volumes ran ~40% below the 20-day average. Weak US jobs data supported the "no further Fed hikes" trade — a constructive global tape into Monday's HK open, albeit on thin volume. [Sina]
