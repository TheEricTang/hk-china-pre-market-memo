Morning Market Memo | 29 Jun 2026 | HK/China Pre-Open

Overnight setup: a memory-led semis rout dragged the Nasdaq to a 5th straight down day and crushed Asian tech Friday, so HK/A-share AI-hardware and storage names open under pressure — even as onshore policy flow stays supportive.

  1. 01

    Global semis rout leads the tape: Friday's US session saw the Nasdaq fall a 5th straight day (‑0.24% to 25,298; week ‑4.6%), Dow ‑0.09%, S&P ‑0.05%, with the Philadelphia Semiconductor Index ‑5.29%. onsemi fell 24% (worst since 2020), Western Digital ‑13%, Seagate ‑12%, SanDisk ‑10%, NXP and Qualcomm ‑7%, Micron ‑6%, Nvidia ‑1.6%; the trigger was a report OpenAI may delay its IPO, reviving AI-capex sustainability worries. Asia was hit harder — SoftBank ‑12%, Korea's KOSPI ‑5.81% to 8,411. Read-through to HK/A hardware (SMIC 0981 HK, Hua Hong 1347 HK) and optical-module/PCB names. [Sina]

  2. 02

    Memory squeeze cuts both ways: Apple and Microsoft raised Mac, iPad and Xbox prices, blaming an AI-driven memory shortage; Micron's CEO says supply may not catch demand until 2028 and PC DDR5 has tripled in a year. Samsung is set to unveil a KRW 1,000tn (~US$651bn) decade-long plan and SK Hynix a US$29bn Nasdaq raise. Positive for A-share storage, though the market is now trading cost-squeeze on downstream margins. [Sina]

  3. 03

    Apple–CXMT sourcing (US-China tech): Apple is reportedly lobbying the White House and Commerce Department for approval to buy memory chips from China's ChangXin Memory (CXMT) to control costs — a potential demand catalyst for the domestic storage chain if cleared. [Eastmoney]

  4. 04

    PBOC liquidity care into half-year-end: the central bank will add overnight reverse repos to open-market operations today and tomorrow (29–30 Jun) to smooth month/half-year funding. This follows a RMB 500bn 1-yr MLF on 25 Jun (net +RMB 200bn) and a RMB 370.5bn 7-day reverse repo at 1.40%. Supportive for rates/liquidity-sensitive sectors. [STCN] [STCN]

  5. 05

    Industrial profits stay strong: Jan–May national above-scale industrial profits rose 18.8% YoY to RMB 3.14tn; electronics profit jumped 103.9% and electronic-special-materials 665.4%, nonferrous smelting +117.1%, while autos ‑19.8% and ferrous smelting ‑37.4%. Confirms the hard-tech / upstream-materials earnings tilt into mid-year reports. [Eastmoney]

  6. 06

    New energy system 15th Five-Year Plan: NDRC and the National Energy Administration issued the plan targeting initial build-out by 2030, with 12 indicators (energy output capacity 5.8bn tce, non-fossil share 25%, power-unit carbon ‑>10%). NEA head Wang Hongzhi said key energy and new-business investment will top RMB 20tn, stressing oil-gas security, new power grids and AI–energy "compute-power coordination." Read-through: grid, renewables, gas, data-center power. [Sina]

  7. 07

    Power semiconductors super-cycle: domestic power-semi makers lifted prices for a second time this year, with order books "stretched four to five months out" and lines running two shifts. Tightness supports IGBT/power-device names. [Eastmoney]

  8. 08

    Compute-power exports surge: Huazhong Zhengyuan, a core unit of HG Tech (000988 CH), saw 800G+ optical-module exports rise more than 100x YoY; fiberglass/electronic-cloth maker China Jushi (600176 CH) is running flat-out. Reinforces the AI-hardware export theme even amid the global selloff. [Eastmoney]

  9. 09

    Piotech M&A, stock halted: Piotech (688072 CH) plans to buy control of Wuxi Shangji (thin-film deposition and etch equipment) via shares plus cash; the stock is suspended from today for up to 10 sessions. Piotech is up ~5x in a year and posted 2025 revenue of RMB 6.52bn (+58.9%). Adds to the semiconductor-equipment consolidation story. [Eastmoney]

  10. 10

    Mid-year earnings and crowding: 19 listed firms issued H1 pre-announcements, several guiding net profit up >100%, feeding the "earnings-delivery" rotation. But positioning is stretched — main capital fled the "optical trio" Eoptolink/Zhongji Innolight/T&S (300502/300308/300394) by >RMB 26.7bn last week, with CATL (300750) also ‑RMB 3.5bn; ~980 stocks now sit below their Sept-2024 pre-rally levels. [Eastmoney] [Eastmoney]

  11. 11

    Regulators tighten: the CSRC handed down a record ~RMB 60mn penalty on two Shenzhen private funds (Qianhai Jiuying AM, Qianhai Tengchuang). SSE flagged Changyingtong, CSSC Special Gas and Yunzhong Tech; SZSE flagged Xingye Leather — a hot-concept speculation reminder. [Eastmoney]

  12. 12

    Consumption support continues: the third batch of RMB 62.5bn in ultra-long special treasury funds for consumer-goods trade-ins ("guobu") has been disbursed, sustaining the appliance/auto/electronics replacement cycle. [Eastmoney]

  13. 13

    HK IPO boom (China–HK): HKEX ran a "super listing week" — 11 firms raised ~HK$25.1bn, six gongs on 26 Jun alone (Lingyi 1688, SG Micro 3661, Chipmore 9630 among them). H1 2026 saw 84 listings (+100% YoY) raising ~US$26.8bn (+92%), a five-year high and #2 globally behind Nasdaq; 121 A-share firms are queued for A+H listings. [Sina]

  14. 14

    Sell-side, ten-broker weekly: consensus is the AI "huddle" won't end easily but is "narrowing." CITIC sticks with AI + energy/chemicals (storage, gas turbines, semis equipment; tin/copper/tungsten); Galaxy pivots to earnings delivery and hard-tech leaders; CSC flags three Q3 swing factors (AI capex, the July Politburo meeting, liquidity). Several houses add low-valuation brokers and dividend names for balance. [Eastmoney]

  15. 15

    Gold extends its slide (commodities): spot gold broke below US$4,000 on 24 Jun — the first "3-handle" since Nov 2025 — down ~30% from the 28 Jan record US$5,598, trading near US$4,046 on 26 Jun. ICBC, CCB, CMB and GF Bank are winding down personal Shanghai-Gold-Exchange agency precious-metals trading over late June–July, accelerating banks' retreat from gold derivatives. [Sina]

  16. 16

    Oil and the Hormuz wildcard (commodities/geopolitics): WTI Aug settled ‑3.7% at US$69.23 and Brent ‑4.3% at US$71.99 Friday, down >10% on the week as Gulf exports rebounded to ~75% of pre-war levels and ships kept transiting Hormuz. But over the weekend the US struck Iran again (27 Jun), Iran skipped the 28 Jun US-Iran technical talks, and the Combined Maritime Information Center raised the Hormuz threat level from "moderate" to "high" — so watch for a firmer oil open. [Sina] [Eastmoney]

  17. 17

    Component inflation and the week ahead: a wave of price hikes takes effect 1 Jul — Murata MLCC +10–40% and Panasonic SP-Cap +5–30%, plus Hikvision HDDs — adding to the passive-component up-cycle. Diary: June PMI on 30 Jun, the Beijing Space Computing Power Conference 29–30 Jun, and lithium-carbonate futures open to overseas traders from 3 Jul. [Eastmoney]
