Morning Market Memo | 30 Jun 2026 | HK/China Pre-Open

- **US overnight – tech-led rally sets a firm tone:** US stocks closed sharply higher Monday after Washington and Tehran agreed to stand down and hold technical talks in Doha today, with the Nasdaq 100 +2.3%, the S&P 500 +1.2% and the Dow at a record. Chips led (VanEck Semiconductor ETF ~+3%); Tesla +8.5%, Alphabet +5% on its first day as a Dow member (replacing Verizon), Amazon +3.2%, Meta +2.2% and Nvidia +1.3% — a supportive read-through for HK/China tech and semis at the open. [[TheStreet](https://www.thestreet.com/stock-market-today/stock-market-today-dow-jones-sp-500-nasdaq-updates-june-29-2026)]

  1. 01

    **PBOC – debut overnight reverse repo adds quarter-end cash:** The central bank ran its first-ever overnight reverse repo on 29 Jun, injecting RMB300bn (fixed-rate, quantity tender), plus RMB157.5bn of 7-day reverse repo at 1.40%. It did not publish the overnight rate; market sources put it at 1.25%, 15bp below the 7-day policy rate and under the 1.35% survey median — read by some as de facto easing. The new tool, flagged by Governor Pan Gongsheng at the Lujiazui Forum, follows last week's RMB500bn 1-yr MLF (a net RMB200bn add) and is aimed at smoothing cross-quarter liquidity. [Sina] [Sina]

  2. 02

    **Memory chips – Apple price hikes and CXMT lobbying underline the super-cycle:** Apple raised Mac, iPad and other hardware prices ~20% globally (25 Jun), blaming surging memory/storage costs, and is reportedly lobbying Washington to approve buying DRAM from China's CXMT (ChangXin/长鑫). CXMT has guided H1 revenue to RMB110–120bn (+612–677% YoY); industry DRAM/NAND prices rose 386%/207% in 2025. Mainland silicon-wafer and storage names extended gains Monday. [Sina] [Sina]

  3. 03

    **Onshore close – semis and innovative drugs lead:** Monday the Shanghai Composite rose 1.16% to 4,073.9 and the ChiNext 0.54%, with chip and storage stocks strong and 60-plus innovative-drug names limit-up. The Hang Seng gained 1.57% to 23,026.7 and the Hang Seng Tech 3.23% on HK$315bn turnover; CSPC (1093 HK) +6.9%, WuXi Bio (2269 HK) +6.8% and Innovent (1801 HK) +6.5% led, while Lenovo (992 HK) -9.2% lagged. [Sina] [Sina]

  4. 04

    **HK IPOs – "super week" with six listings in a day:** 22–28 Jun saw 10 new listings, 19 fresh filings and 14 hearings cleared; on 26 Jun six firms debuted together — among the most crowded listing days in HK history — including SG Micro (圣邦股份, +47%), Lingyi iTech (领益智造), 科拓股份 (+200%) and 中科闻歌 (+84%). Mainland leaders Luxshare (立讯精密), Megmeet (麦格米特) and Kidswant (孩子王) filed for "A-to-H" listings. [STCN]

  5. 05

    **HK IPOs – H1 a record HK$200bn-plus:** First-half Hong Kong IPO proceeds topped HK$200bn across roughly 84 deals, on track for the best year in years, with AI and semiconductor names the dominant supply; about 17 firms list in the 22–30 Jun window alone. [STCN]

  6. 06

    **Sell-side – Goldman stays bullish on Xiaomi:** Goldman reiterated Buy on Xiaomi (1810 HK) with a 12-month target of HK$40, pointing to a catalyst-rich few months ahead across its EV ramp and new launches. [Sina]

  7. 07

    **Buybacks – Tencent keeps repurchasing:** Tencent (700 HK) bought back HK$501m of stock on 24 Jun, a 26th consecutive session of repurchases, sustaining one of the market's largest buyback programmes. [STCN]

  8. 08

    **Gold – fourth straight weekly drop on a hawkish Fed:** Gold sits near $4,050/oz, roughly 30% below its January peak and down for a fourth week running. The pressure is policy-led — new Fed Chair Kevin Warsh's anti-inflation stance, with US inflation above 4% and markets now pricing rate hikes rather than cuts — compounded by a fading Middle East war-risk premium. [TradingEconomics] [Sina] [CNBC]

  9. 09

    **Oil – Brent firms but stays well below the war peak:** Brent (Aug) traded near $73, up ~0.9% Monday, after weekend US–Iran strikes revived doubts over Strait of Hormuz shipping; the two sides hold technical talks in Doha today. Crude remains about 20% below its 2026 wartime high — relevant for CNOOC, PetroChina and Sinopec and for energy index weights. [Al Jazeera]

  10. 10

    **NPC – trademark law revised; HK gains Huanggang Port jurisdiction:** The 14th NPC Standing Committee closed its 23rd session on 26 Jun, passing a revised Trademark Law and a decision to amend the CPA Law, and authorising Hong Kong to exercise jurisdiction over the Huanggang Port HK port area — a step for Shenzhen–HK cross-boundary integration. It also removed Guan Zhi'ou as natural-resources minister and approved the 2025 central accounts. [Xinhua]

  11. 11

    **Data – June PMI due at the open:** China's June official manufacturing and non-manufacturing PMIs are released by the NBS at 09:30 HKT today; May manufacturing printed 50.0, right at the expansion line and down 0.3pt, so today's reading is a key gauge of quarter-end momentum. [NBS]

  12. 12

    **Regulation – ChiNext trading rules take effect 6 Jul:** Revised ChiNext trading rules tied to the broader ChiNext reform take effect next Monday (6 Jul), following the STAR Market "eight measures" (19 Jun) and the SSE "Quality & Returns 2.0" return-on-capital campaign — a continued push to lift listed-company quality and shareholder returns. [STCN]
