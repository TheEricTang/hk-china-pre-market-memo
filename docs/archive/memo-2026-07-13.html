Morning Market Memo | 13 Jul 2026 | HK/China Pre-Open

(covers 10 Jul 16:00 HKT close → 13 Jul 9:30 HKT open)

  1. 01

    Hormuz closed, US strikes continue: Iran's IRGC navy announced early Sunday the Strait of Hormuz is closed to all shipping "until further notice" after firing warning shots at a vessel it says ignored routing instructions; US CENTCOM began its fourth round of strikes on Iran this week at 17:00 EDT Sunday (early Monday HKT), aimed at Iran's ability to attack commercial shipping. NY crude off-hours trade was up 3.27% (Brent +3.10%) as of Sunday morning, from Friday's WTI settle of $71.51 (-0.79%); Oman has drafted a two-corridor transit scheme but nothing is confirmed. Energy producers, tankers/shipping and airline fuel costs are the open's main read-through. [STCN] [Eastmoney]

  2. 02

    Overnight US markets (Friday close): Dow +0.29% at 52,637.01, S&P 500 +0.42% at 7,575.39, Nasdaq +0.29% at 26,281.61 (week: Nasdaq +1.74%, S&P +1.23%); materials led sectors (+1.12%), healthcare lagged (-0.81%). SK Hynix's ADR debut jumped 12.76% to a $1.22tn market cap, overtaking Micron; Nvidia +4%, SanDisk +3%, Applied Materials/Seagate/AMD +2%, while Intel/UMC fell over 2% and Micron slipped 1%. Nasdaq Golden Dragon -0.23%: JD, Li Auto and Alibaba up ~1%, Tencent ADR -1%, NetEase -4%. Direct read-through for the HK memory/AI-hardware chain (SMIC 981 HK, Hua Hong 1347 HK) at the open. [Xinhua] [STCN]

  3. 03

    Cross-assets (Friday settles, all pre-dating the Hormuz closure): COMEX gold -0.29% at $4,128.9/oz (spot $4,119.48, holding above $4,100); COMEX silver $60.3. LME copper $13,484/t; WTI $71.51. US 10-yr 4.5612% (+7.8bp on the week), 2-yr 4.2079%; DXY 100.953. Onshore CNY closed 6.7784, up 15 pips; overnight Shibor 1.3601%. [Eastmoney]

  4. 04

    State Council executive meeting (Friday evening): Li Qiang chaired a meeting calling for systematic advancement of Digital China, "moderately ahead-of-demand" digital infrastructure, and faster build-out of next-generation communication and computing-power networks; it also studied cultivating new pillar industries via full-chain scale-up and key software/hardware breakthroughs, and approved the National Fitness Plan (2026–2030). CCTV Finance separately reported a forecast that domestic compute-card procurement will reach RMB1.44tn by 2029. Positive for telecom equipment, IDC and compute-infrastructure names. [STCN] [Eastmoney]

  5. 05

    Helium export ban (critical materials): MOFCOM and the Customs Administration announced at 18:22 Friday a temporary ban on helium exports (HS 2804290010), effective immediately. Helium — dubbed the "gas chip" — is essential to semiconductor manufacturing, MRI and aerospace; China's supply gap is put at over 60% amid a global shortage driven by Qatar/Russia disruptions, and domestic prices have been rising daily. Supports specialty-gas producers and extends the critical-materials export toolbox alongside rare-earth controls. [Sina/Xinhua Finance] [STCN]

  6. 06

    China–HK regulatory cooperation: the CSRC and Hong Kong SFC held their 17th joint enforcement work meeting, pledging to strengthen cross-border enforcement cooperation, intelligence sharing and a crackdown on new types of cross-border violations under the new "Nine Measures" framework. Relevant for cross-listed names and brokers active in both markets. [STCN]

  7. 07

    New issues — CXMT headlines the week: 长鑫科技 (CXMT, 688825 CH), the domestic DRAM maker, opens subscription Thursday 16 Jul, raising RMB29.5bn — the largest A-share IPO this year and the second-largest STAR Market IPO ever; 维琪科技 subscribes today (Beijing Stock Exchange). The CSRC also cleared STAR-board IPO registration for AI-chip maker Shanghai Enflame (燧原科技) plus two other companies. Expect funding-rotation effects in semis into the CXMT print. [STCN]

  8. 08

    Memory shortage narrative extends: SK Hynix's CEO, in his first interview after the record US listing, said the memory-chip shortage hitting PCs, autos and consumer electronics will likely persist beyond 2030, with customers signing long-term contracts; Samsung is separately reported to be pulling forward its first Yongin fab to 2029, one to two years early. Storage distributor 香农芯创 300475 CH guided H1 net profit to RMB3.5–4.0bn, up 2,118–2,434% y/y (implied Q2 +63–101% q/q). Keeps the HK/A storage chain in focus at the open. [Eastmoney]

  9. 09

    CATL-linked restructuring resumes trading: 日科化学 300214 CH resumes today after disclosing Friday evening a plan to acquire 70.75% of Shandong Genyuan New Materials via shares plus cash from 27 counterparties including CATL 300750 CH, with a matching private placement; the deal constitutes a major asset restructuring and related-party transaction. [STCN]

  10. 10

    Central-SOE reform completed: Sinopec (386 HK / 600028 CH) announced Saturday it has completed the absorption of China National Aviation Fuel, now a wholly-owned tier-2 subsidiary; all domestic and overseas approvals cleared 18 Jun and registration changed 9 Jul. The deal integrates refining with the national jet-fuel supply network — China's aviation-fuel consumption is projected to grow from 39.28m tonnes (2024) to ~75m tonnes by 2040. [Xinhua] [STCN]

  11. 11

    TCM "15th Five-Year" plan approved: the State Council published (21:39 Friday) its reply approving the Traditional Chinese Medicine Revitalisation Development Plan, requiring provinces to treat TCM revitalisation as a key task and pushing TCM modernisation and internationalisation. Positive for the HK/A TCM complex. [Sina/经济参考报]

  12. 12

    H1 pre-announcements (Friday/Sunday evenings): CITIC Securities 600030 CH guided H1 net profit to RMB23.34bn, +69.6% y/y (implied Q2 +28% q/q) — a strong marker for the brokerage complex; 紫光股份 000938 CH +83.5–122.9%; 杉杉股份 600884 CH +262–334% on anode materials and polarisers; tungsten producer 翔鹭钨业 002842 CH +2,348–3,436%. On the loss side, Seres 601127 CH guided to an H1 loss of RMB1.5–1.8bn (swinging from profit), and hog producer Muyuan 002714 CH to a RMB5.7–6.7bn loss with average hog prices down ~28% y/y. [Eastmoney] [Eastmoney]

  13. 13

    Power load at record high: the NDRC said national power load hit 1.518bn kW on 10 Jul, the first record of the year and 10GW above the previous peak, on widespread high temperatures and rising electrification. Relevance for grid equipment, IPPs and coal supply into peak summer. [Eastmoney]

  14. 14

    Gold/nonferrous SOE consolidation in Xinjiang: 西部黄金 601069 CH said Sunday evening its controlling shareholder Xinjiang Nonferrous Metals Group will merge with Xinjiang Geology & Mining Investment Group; the actual controller (Xinjiang SASAC) is unchanged and operations are unaffected. Watch for consolidation read-through across regional gold/nonferrous names. [Eastmoney]

  15. 15

    Commercial-space theme discipline: 星网宇达 002829 CH (two straight limit-ups) clarified Sunday evening that its satellite-communication business has no link to the Long March-10B sea-net recovery breakthrough, with the speculated businesses under 5% of revenue; 巨力索具 002342 CH was fined RMB4.5m by the Hebei CSRC bureau for misleading statements about commercial-aerospace products and orders. Cooling signals for last week's hottest theme. [Eastmoney]

  16. 16

    Week ahead and positioning: June activity data (industrial output, FAI, retail sales) lands Wednesday 15 Jul; the first A-share interim reports arrive from 16 Jul; new Fed Chair Kevin Warsh gives his first congressional testimony Tuesday 22:00 HKT. About RMB53.6bn of A-share lock-ups expire this week, led by 屹唐股份 (RMB35.2bn). Sunday's sell-side wrap: broad consensus that last week's tech pullback (SSE -1.17%, ChiNext -4.41%) was a de-risking of "high valuation/high crowding" positions within an intact structural bull — Kaiyuan and SWHS see the correction near its end, with SWHS flagging fund-splitting risk around the CXMT listing; Galaxy points to storage/advanced packaging as the earnings-anchored re-entry. [STCN] [Eastmoney]
