Morning Market Memo | 09 Jul 2026 | HK/China Pre-Open

(covers 08 Jul 16:00 HKT close → 09 Jul 9:30 HKT open)

  1. 01

    US–Iran: Trump declares the memorandum "terminated": Speaking at the NATO summit in Ankara at 16:24 HKT Wednesday, Trump said he considers the US–Iran memorandum of understanding — signed 17 June with a 60-day commitment to negotiate a final deal — "已终结" (over), adding he no longer wants to deal with Tehran. Negative for the oil-import complex (airlines, Cathay 293 HK) and supportive for oil producers at today's open. [Eastmoney/CCTV]

At 04:42 HKT today, Iran's Nour News quoted a military source saying missile and drone units would launch large-scale strikes on US bases in the Middle East "within minutes". Fast-moving — verify latest status at the open; read-through to oil names (883 HK, 857 HK) and shipping. [Eastmoney/CCTV]

    Overnight US markets (data): Dow -1.09% at 52,348.39, S&P 500 -0.28% at 7,482.71, Nasdaq +0.2% at 25,870.65. Semis/storage rebounded after Tuesday's rout — SanDisk +6%, Seagate and NXP +~4%, Western Digital +3%, Micron/ASML +1%; Broadcom +~5% on a new multi-year Apple deal worth over US$30bn covering 15bn+ US-made chips; Nvidia +3%, Super Micro +7%, but Meta and Tesla fell 2%+. China ADRs outperformed: Nasdaq Golden Dragon +2.05%, Alibaba +11% extending Wednesday's HK surge, Baidu +~5%, JD +4% — supportive for HK internet at the open (9988/9888/9618 HK). Europe closed sharply lower (DAX -2.23%, CAC -2.18%). Cross-assets: WTI +6.13% to US$74.76; spot gold -0.69% to US$4,077 (COMEX -1.70% to US$4,086.6) and spot silver -2.78% to US$58.3 as strikes hit metals; DXY 100.99; US 10y 4.5611%; onshore CNY closed 6.7993, offshore 6.8052. [Eastmoney] [Eastmoney]

  4. 04

    Fed minutes (published 04:00 HKT): The first minutes under new chair Kevin Warsh show the FOMC held at 3.5%–3.75% but a minority saw grounds to hike in June; upside price risks were judged "elevated", and most participants said policy tightening "may be necessary" if AI demand, Mideast conflict or tariffs keep inflation high. May PCE ran 4.1% y/y, a post-2023 high. Next catalysts: 14 July US CPI and Warsh's first House hearing the same day — a hawkish overhang for HK rate-sensitives. [Eastmoney/Cailianshe]

  5. 05

    ChangXin Memory (CXMT) launches STAR IPO: China's largest DRAM maker published its prospectus early this morning; price inquiry 13 July, subscription 16 July (code 688825), 6.688bn shares or 10% of post-issue capital, with a 15% CICC greenshoe. It guides H1 revenue of RMB110–120bn (+613%–677% y/y) and net profit of RMB50–57bn (+2,244%–2,544%) on DRAM shortages and price rises. Fresh catalyst for the A/HK memory and semi complex; separately, SK Hynix's US$28bn ADR bookbuild closed oversubscribed several times over. [Eastmoney/SSE News] [Eastmoney]

  6. 06

    PBOC Q2 monetary policy committee meeting: The readout calls for large banks to serve the real economy as the "main force", strengthening bank capital, better use of structural tools, and stepped-up financial support for domestic demand expansion, tech innovation and SMEs. Supportive tone for banks and growth-policy plays; no rate signal. [Eastmoney]

    Northbound holdings top RMB3tn for the first time: Q2 exchange data published Wednesday evening show northbound funds held RMB3.13tn (from RMB2.58tn in Q1) across 3,958 stocks, with Q2 net buying of RMB208.6bn. CATL (300750 CH) remains the top holding at RMB351.4bn after RMB55.4bn of net buying (20.28% of its A-shares); electronics holdings jumped RMB405bn (+107%). Foreign positioning tailwind for the tech complex. [Eastmoney]

[Eastmoney]
