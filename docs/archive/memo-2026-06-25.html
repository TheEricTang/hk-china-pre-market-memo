Morning Market Memo | 25 Jun 2026 | HK/China Pre-Open

- Li Qiang opens Summer Davos in Dalian: Premier Li Qiang gave the opening address at the 17th Annual Meeting of the New Champions ("Summer Davos"), themed "innovation at scale," before ~1,800 delegates. He framed 2026 as the first year of the 15th Five-Year Plan, called the economy "stable, new, dynamic and integrated," and pledged wider market access, a first-class business environment and stronger AI global governance — a top-level pro-opening signal for HK-listed China names. [[Xinhua](https://www.news.cn/politics/leaders/20260624/c4f80fc959ca4731a1e602f89600de41/c.html)]

  1. 01

    SOE reform "starting gun": A national video conference on further deepening state-owned-enterprise reform was held in Beijing on 23 Jun, billed as the mobilization meeting that kicks off a new reform round. It stressed fusing tech and industrial innovation, basic research and scenario opening to turn results into productive forces — read-through to central-SOE and SASAC-theme names. [Sina · Securities Times]

  2. 02

    CSRC pushes "tech-enabled" regulation: CSRC chairman Wu Qing, at the Lujiazui Forum, said the regulator will raise its use of technology and AI in supervision and crack down on AI-based illegal stock tipping, rumor-mongering, manipulation and insider trading. Relevant for brokers and fintech. [Sina · Securities Times]

  3. 03

    Public-fund AUM at a record, issuance still cautious: Mutual-fund net assets hit a record RMB39.48tn at end-May per AMAC, while new-fund launches this year raised under half the 2021 pace with few blockbuster equity products — ample on-platform liquidity but restrained primary supply, supportive for asset managers. [Sina · Securities Daily]

  4. 04

    CXMT IPO advances; banks early into DRAM: The SSE listing committee cleared Changxin (CXMT) for a STAR Market IPO on 27 May (a record 148 days from filing) and it is now in registration — potentially China's largest-ever chip listing. Five big-bank AIC units put ~RMB3.9bn into a 2024 round (~36% of an RMB10.8bn raise); Guolian Minsheng estimates CCB's paper gain could reach RMB41bn at a RMB3tn cap. A SemiAnalysis report (23 Jun) called CXMT set to challenge DRAM incumbents — read-through to domestic storage and HK semis. [Sina]

  5. 05

    JCET commits RMB7.8bn to advanced packaging: Packaging/test leader JCET (600584) said on 24 Jun it will build a high-end advanced packaging-and-test plant at Shanghai Lingang's "Oriental Chip Port," total investment RMB7.8bn (new subsidiary capitalized at RMB4bn) — reinforcing the domestic advanced-packaging capex theme. [Sina · China Securities Journal]

  6. 06

    Five ministries launch industrial 5G private-network pilot: MIIT and four other ministries issued a notice rolling out a pilot for industrial 5G standalone private networks, building dedicated comms infrastructure for large and extra-large enterprises and deepening "5G + industrial internet." Relevant for telecom-equipment and industrial-digitalization names (China Mobile, ZTE, integrators). [Sina · Shanghai Securities News]

  7. 07

    AI liquid-cooling routes in focus: Google's "Brazos" and Nvidia's full-liquid-cooling "Rubin" designs have put data-center thermal management back in the spotlight, with A-share firms positioned across the liquid-cooling chain — supportive for cooling-component and data-center supply-chain names. [Sina · Shanghai Securities News]

  8. 08

    ByteDance starts charging for Doubao: ByteDance launched a tiered paid subscription for its Doubao app on the new Doubao 2.1 Pro model — three paid tiers (office/dev, data-analysis, pro-design) plus a free base tier. Marks domestic LLMs shifting toward "free + paid value-add" monetization, incremental for AI-application and compute demand. [Sina · Securities Daily]

  9. 09

    MWC Shanghai opens with 6G and satellite: MWC Shanghai opened on 24 Jun, showcasing 6G sensing-computing-AI integration, a first public "future constellation" zone with a handset-direct test-satellite model, embodied-AI robots and "space-air-ground-sea" network demos. Catalyst for 6G, satellite and telecom-equipment themes. [Sina · Securities Daily]

  10. 10

    Unitree cuts humanoid price: Unitree said on 24 Jun its bipedal humanoid R1 now starts at RMB29,900 with spot sales open, RMB10,000 below the RMB39,900 launch price set in July 2025. The 25kg, 26-joint robot runs a multimodal model — another humanoid cost-down step supporting the robotics supply chain. [Sina · China Securities Journal]

  11. 11

    Alibaba sues the US Defense Department: Alibaba confirmed to Global Times it has sued the US DoD in San Jose federal court (filed 23 Jun), seeking removal from the "Chinese military companies" list after the DoD added Alibaba, Baidu and BYD on 8 Jun. Alibaba denies any military-civil fusion role and argues the listing breaches due process — following Xiaomi, Hesai and WuXi AppTec. Watch BABA/BIDU (9988/9888 HK) and BYD sentiment. [Sina · Global Times]

  12. 12

    Gold/silver slump; banks pull precious-metals desks: Policy/market reaction first — ICBC said it will halt agency individual precious-metals auction trading on the Shanghai Gold Exchange from end-of-day 24 Jul (Au99.99, Au(T+D), Ag(T+D), etc.), as banks de-risk into a sharp selloff. Spot silver broke below US$61/oz intraday on 24 Jun (>50% off its January high) and spot gold slid under US$4,100 (>27% off the January peak) — pressure for gold miners (Zijin, Shandong Gold) and bank precious-metals fee income. [Sina]

  13. 13

    Shipping/shipbuilding boom seen extending into H2: Brokers expect elevated freight and shipbuilding activity to persist as Hormuz disruption lingers; Iran's UN Geneva envoy said on 23 Jun the strait is open to merchant vessels for 60 days free of charge, though access is conditional and navigation risk not fully cleared. Supportive for tanker and shipbuilding names (COSCO Shipping, CSSC); treat "fully open" as condition-dependent. [Sina · Shanghai Securities News]

  14. 14

    China innovative-drug read on Pfizer setback: Pfizer's first-in-class ADC sigvotatug vedotin missed the primary endpoint in its Phase III SigVie-002 trial (22 Jun), with the stock down 1.44% on 23 Jun, prompting "valley of death" commentary on clinical R&D risk. A clinical-risk reminder, but also a relative read for China's innovative-drug and CRO/CDMO names (WuXi, etc.) in the ADC space. [Sina · Securities Times]

  15. 15

    Global / overnight — Micron blowout underscores memory super-cycle: Micron's fiscal-Q3 revenue more than quadrupled YoY to US$41.46bn (vs US$35.84bn est), adjusted EPS US$25.11, gross margin ~84.9% and next-quarter guidance ~US$50bn; shares rose ~10% after hours on memory tightness it sees persisting beyond 2027. Strong read-through to China DRAM/storage (CXMT theme) and HK semis (SMIC, Hua Hong). [Sina]
