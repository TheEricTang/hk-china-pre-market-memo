Morning Market Memo | 26 Jun 2026 | HK/China Pre-Open

Dominant overnight event: Micron's blowout confirmed the memory/storage super-cycle, lifting US chip names to record closes and validating the A-share storage rally — but megacaps (Apple, China ADRs) fell, so the read-through is selective.

  1. 01

    **Storage super-cycle – Micron's "王炸" print:** AI-memory leader Micron posted FQ3 (Mar–May) revenue up 346% YoY to US$41.46bn, gross margin 84.9% and adjusted EPS US$25.11 (vs ~$20.28 est), and guided FQ4 revenue to ~US$50bn. It has locked ~US$22bn of long-term customer commitments and said HBM4 12-high is ramping twice as fast as HBM3E (>US$1bn HBM4 shipped); CEO Mehrotra sees shortages persisting into 2028. Shares rose up to 16% after-hours. Direct read-through to HK/China memory and storage-chain names. [Eastmoney]

  2. 02

    **Onshore storage/semis already ripping:** A-shares closed sharply higher Thu, ChiNext +2.84% (4,371.99) and STAR50 +3.87% (2,066.33) both at record highs; SSE +0.23%, turnover RMB3.62trn (+RMB312bn). Storage-chip names exploded — Ingenic (300223) +20% limit, Demingli limit-up; advanced packaging (JCET) and CPO/optical also led, with RMB11.5bn net inflow to components. Micron's print is a fresh catalyst at the open. [Eastmoney]

  3. 03

    **AI memory inflation hits hardware names:** Apple formally raised MacBook/iPad prices by up to US$300 (CEO Cook had warned soaring memory/storage costs would force hikes) and Microsoft lifted Xbox console prices, citing storage and memory costs up ~2.5x. Apple closed -6%+ (biggest drop since Apr 2025), Microsoft -3%+. Negative for Apple-chain assemblers' margins, but reinforces pricing power for memory, MLCC and PCB/CCL suppliers. [Eastmoney]

  4. 04

    **Sell-side – Citi turns bullish on optical:** Citi raised Eoptolink (300502) target ~98% to RMB701 and lifted Dongshan Precision (002384) and T&S Communications (300394), seeing the global optical-interconnect market at ~US$92bn by 2028 (3-yr CAGR 65%) on 3.2T/NPO migration. Supports CPO/optical-module and AIDC names. [Eastmoney]

  5. 05

    **PBOC adds overnight reverse repo for month-end:** The PBOC said 25 Jun it will add an overnight reverse-repo instrument in open-market operations on 29–30 Jun (fixed rate, quantity tender), implementing Gov. Pan Gongsheng's 17 Jun Lujiazui pledge to enrich the toolbox and narrow the temporary repo band to the 7-day rate ±25bp. Brokers say it fills a maturity gap and gives more precise quarter-end liquidity control, with overnight rates seen converging to 1.30–1.40% and a possible longer-term shift to an overnight-rate-led framework. Supportive for banks/funding into quarter-end. [Eastmoney]

  6. 06

    **US–China – trade council, but fresh retaliation:** MOFCOM spokesman He Yadong said 25 Jun that, per the consensus reached, both sides agreed to set up a Trade Council and will discuss reciprocal tariff cuts under it, with teams holding further talks; aircraft and farm-goods trade called "mutually beneficial." Separately, China on 22 Jun added 10 US entities (incl. Avioxx) to its export-control list and barred government procurement from 46 US firms, retaliating against the US "Chinese military companies" listing. Constructive tone on tariffs, but the friction track continues. [Eastmoney]

  7. 07

    **Industrial policy – new energy 15th-Five-Year plan:** NDRC and the National Energy Administration issued the New Energy System Construction "15th Five-Year" Plan (25 Jun): by 2030 non-fossil energy to reach 50% of power generation, wind+solar >50% of installed capacity, nuclear ~110GW and hydro ~410GW in operation, new-type storage 300GW plus pumped-hydro 160GW, and EV charging points to 40mn. Positive for grid, renewables, nuclear, storage and lithium/silicon names. [Eastmoney]

  8. 08

    **Xi in Shandong:** Xi Jinping inspected Dezhou, Shandong (24 Jun), stressing that agricultural and rural modernization "bears on the overall picture of Chinese-style modernization," urging stable grain/key farm-product supply, support policies, and rollout of advanced seed varieties, technology and equipment. He Lifeng accompanied. Read-through to seed/agriculture and food-security names. [Chinanews]

  9. 09

    **Li Qiang opens Summer Davos:** Premier Li Qiang addressed the 2026 Summer Davos in Dalian (theme "scaled innovation"), describing the economy as "stable, new, vibrant, integrated" in the 15th-Five-Year opening year, pitching "China Opportunity 2.0," and pledging wider market access, a first-class business environment and constructive AI global governance. Positive for foreign-invested and China A/H sentiment. [MFA]

  10. 10

    **Tencent (700 HK) – WeChat AI roll-out, GS cautious:** Tencent keeps shipping WeChat-ecosystem AI — Enterprise WeChat's "大圆" agent entered internal testing, the fourth WeChat-AI item this month (after native assistant "小微," WeChat Pay AI card, open AI access). Goldman Sachs (23 Jun) flagged that "小微" runs on in-house WeLM rather than Hunyuan, with monetization unclear near-term. Tencent's ADR fell ~3% in Thu US trade (Golden Dragon -2.70%; Alibaba -4%+, PDD -3%+) — a likely drag on HK tech at the open. [Eastmoney]

  11. 11

    **Compute/HBM peers – Qualcomm, SK Hynix:** Qualcomm used its investor day to push into AI data-center compute (new DC CPU, an AI-software buy, raised guidance), shares +13% after-hours; SK Hynix jumped on plans for a US ADR listing to close its HBM-leader valuation gap. Reinforces the AI compute + HBM narrative for HK/China supply-chain and equipment names. [Eastmoney]

  12. 12

    **Oil – modest bounce after the slide:** WTI front-month settled +1.61% at US$71.47 Thu (Brent ~US$74), a bounce after a multi-session slide that left crude ~26% lower over the month on Middle East de-escalation. Trump ordered a DOJ probe into alleged gasoline "gouging"; Chevron's CFO sees only "gradual" pump-price declines. Watch PetroChina/Sinopec and oil-services. [Fortune] [Eastmoney]

  13. 13

    **Gold – steadying at the US$4,000 line:** COMEX gold settled +0.82% at US$4,041.6 and spot +0.69% at US$4,026.78 Thu, firming back above US$4,000 after dipping under it earlier in the week; silver ~US$57.9. Neutral-to-supportive for HK/A-share gold miners (Zijin, Zhaojin). [Eastmoney]

  14. 14

    **US PCE / Fed signal:** May headline PCE rose 0.4% m/m and 4.1% y/y — the highest since Apr 2023 — but landed at/below expectations, so markets took relief; the IMF backed last week's Fed hold. A still-firm print keeps the bar high for near-term easing, a factor for HK rates-sensitive and property names. [Eastmoney]

  15. 15

    **Sell-side – China-tech crowding flagged:** Huatai noted Chinese AI assets led globally over the past fortnight but "apparent crowding is now high," advising tighter position control and focus on domestic models, optical modules, storage/CCL and MLCC. CICC said the Middle East ceasefire should repair sentiment and favored low-positioned physical-AI/robotics and auto-export plays. A note of caution after the semis surge. [Eastmoney]
