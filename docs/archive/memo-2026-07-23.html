Morning Market Memo | 23 Jul 2026 | HK/China Pre-Open

(covers 22 Jul 2026 16:00 HKT close → 23 Jul 2026 08:10 HKT research cutoff)

  1. 01

    US markets – tech and China ADRs softer: The Dow closed down 0.01%, S&P 500 fell 0.14% and Nasdaq lost 0.57%. Nvidia rose more than 2% and Super Micro Computer jumped nearly 20%, while Micron fell more than 1%; the Nasdaq Golden Dragon index dropped 1.80%, with NetEase down over 6%, Bilibili over 3% and Alibaba over 1%. [Eastmoney]

  2. 02

    Cross-assets – oil and precious metals higher: WTI settled 2.54% higher at US$86.48/bbl as Hormuz tensions persisted; COMEX gold gained 1.44% to US$4,135/oz and silver rose 1.57% to US$60.04/oz. The DXY eased 0.05% to 101.125, while US two- and ten-year yields rose to 4.3042% and 4.6626%, respectively. [Eastmoney]

  3. 03

    Hormuz – Iran threatens wider energy disruption: Iran’s military said the strait remained closed except for vessels using designated routes and warned that a US strike on Iranian infrastructure would put regional oil, gas and power assets at risk. The escalation keeps the focus on PetroChina, CNOOC, Sinopec and airline fuel costs. [Eastmoney]

  4. 04

    Alphabet – capex raised again: Second-quarter revenue rose 24% to US$119.8bn and Google Cloud revenue increased 82% to US$24.8bn, but Alphabet lifted its 2026 capital-spending forecast to US$195bn–205bn from US$180bn–190bn. Shares fell about 4% after hours; continued server and data-centre spending remains relevant to Chinese optics, PCB, power-equipment and cooling suppliers. [Eastmoney]

  5. 05

    Tesla – revenue beat, margins and cash flow miss: Second-quarter revenue increased 26% to US$28.24bn, but adjusted EPS of US$0.33 missed the US$0.51 estimate and free cash flow was negative US$1.09bn. The shares fell as much as 5% after hours, providing a mixed read-through for BYD, Li Auto, XPeng, NIO and the battery chain. [Eastmoney]

  6. 06

    Global AI infrastructure – spending plans expand: OpenAI reportedly raised its projected computing expenditure through 2030 from about US$600bn to US$750bn and plans to spend more than US$30bn on new data centres. Microsoft also signed a multibillion-dollar data-centre agreement with Mistral, while Anthropic plans to buy up to two gigawatts of AMD’s latest chips from 2027. [Eastmoney] [Eastmoney]

  7. 07

    Beijing state capital – more equity purchases planned: Beijing State-owned Capital Operation and Management said it has deployed nearly RMB10bn of proprietary funds into equities and will continue adding listed-company shares through its own capital, securities subsidiary and public-fund platform. It also pledged support for Beijing Stock Exchange development. [ET Net] [JW View]

  8. 08

    Central SOEs – reform and “AI+” remain priorities: SASAC’s central-enterprise leadership meeting set six second-half tasks, including deeper restructuring, core-technology research, industrial upgrading and risk control. It called for an AI-for-scientific-research programme, more high-value application scenarios and faster development of emerging industries. [Eastmoney]

  9. 09

    Jerry – US data-centre power order: Jerry’s 002353 CH subsidiary accepted a US$1.465bn, or roughly RMB9.95bn, gas-turbine generator order from an unnamed global cloud provider, equal to 61.33% of 2025 revenue. Delivery is scheduled in batches by November 2027, expanding the company beyond traditional oilfield equipment into data-centre power. [Eastmoney]

  10. 10

    A-share buybacks – storage and infrastructure names act: Baiwei Storage’s chairman proposed an RMB200m–250m repurchase for cancellation; Daqin Railway proposed RMB400m–500m for cancellation, while Enjie plans RMB100m–200m for employee incentives or share ownership. The post-close announcements add company-level support across storage, transport and battery materials. [Eastmoney]

  11. 11

    Brokers – sector-wide stabilisation purchases: Changjiang Securities’ chairman proposed an RMB100m–200m buyback and Industrial Securities’ shareholder Fujian Investment plans to add RMB30m–60m over six months. Together with previously announced programmes, broker-sector repurchases and increases have a combined upper limit of RMB1.26bn. [Eastmoney]

  12. 12

    Dongshan Precision – optics theft impact limited: Dongshan Precision 002384 CH confirmed that optical-module inventory was stolen from an overseas warehouse but rejected online claims that the loss ran into tens of millions of US dollars. The company said the profit impact was limited and below the disclosure threshold. [Sina] [Eastmoney]

  13. 13

    Semiconductor results – equipment and analogue names deliver: Lianxun Instruments expects first-half net profit of RMB510m–580m, up 802%–926%, while Zhongwei Semiconductor forecasts RMB165m, up 90.8%. The evening releases support the domestic semiconductor-equipment and chip-testing complex. [Eastmoney] [Eastmoney]

  14. 14

    Hengtong Optic-Electric – employee incentive purchase completed: Hengtong bought 1.851m shares through its employee-incentive trust on 22 July, using RMB108m of award funds. The shares represent about 0.08% of outstanding capital, completing the company’s 2025 award-fund purchase. [Eastmoney]

  15. 15

    PCB supply chain – high-end capacity remains tight: CCTV reported that high-layer PCB plants serving AI servers are running at full utilisation, with customers finding it difficult to insert new orders; tight high-end copper-clad laminate supply is also raising production costs. The read-through covers Shennan Circuits, Wus Printed Circuit, Victory Giant and Kingboard Laminates. [Eastmoney]

  16. 16

    Southbound flows – ETFs and foundries bought: Southbound investors recorded RMB7.52bn of net purchases on Wednesday. Tracker Fund and Hua Hong Semiconductor led Shanghai-connect buying, while Tracker Fund and SMIC led Shenzhen-connect inflows; Alibaba and Tencent were the largest respective net sells. [NBD]
