Morning Market Memo | 23 Jul 2026 | HK/China Pre-Open

(covers 22 Jul 2026 16:00 HKT close → 23 Jul 2026 06:20 HKT research cutoff)

  1. 01

    **US close – Nasdaq lagged; AI hardware outperformed:** The Dow slipped 0.01%, S&P 500 fell 0.14% and Nasdaq lost 0.57%. Nvidia rose over 2%, AMD gained over 1%, Super Micro jumped nearly 20% and Dell added over 9%, while Micron fell over 1%; the China ADR wrap highlighted NetEase down more than 6%. [Eastmoney]

  2. 02

    **Alphabet – stronger cloud, heavier AI spending:** Q2 revenue rose 24% to US$119.8bn and Google Cloud revenue reached US$24.77bn, both ahead of estimates. Alphabet raised 2026 capital-spending guidance to US$195bn–205bn; the shares fell more than 3% after hours, relevant for China cloud, data-centre and optical-component names. [Eastmoney]

  3. 03

    **Tesla – sales beat but margins missed:** Q2 revenue increased 26% to US$28.24bn, but adjusted EPS of US$0.33 missed the US$0.51 estimate and free cash flow turned negative at US$1.09bn. The shares fell as much as 5% after hours, providing a softer read-through for the HK/A EV complex. [Eastmoney]

  4. 04

    **Oil and metals – Hormuz tension lifted commodities:** WTI settled 2.54% higher at US$86.48 a barrel; spot gold rose 1.28% to US$4,130.09 and silver gained 1.58% to US$59.73. Iran said the Hormuz Strait remained closed under its designated procedures and threatened regional energy infrastructure if the US attacks, keeping oil producers, airlines and precious-metal names in focus. [Eastmoney]

  5. 05

    **Rates and FX – Treasury yields rose:** The US 10-year yield increased 3.45bp to 4.6626% and the two-year rose 4.27bp to 4.3042%. The DXY edged 0.05% lower to 101.125, while onshore yuan finished Wednesday at 6.7740 per dollar. [Eastmoney]

  6. 06

    **CSRC – foreign participation and market stability:** Chairman Wu Qing said the regulator would firmly maintain stable and healthy capital-market development, expand institutional opening and improve convenience for foreign investors. CPP Investments CEO John Graham said China remained an important global investment region and the fund would continue allocating to the country. [CSRC]

  7. 07

    **Beijing state capital – further equity purchases planned:** Beijing State-owned Capital Operation and Management said it had deployed nearly RMB10bn of proprietary funds into the stock market. It plans further purchases through its own capital, securities and fund subsidiaries, while supporting listed companies and Beijing Stock Exchange development. [Eastmoney]

  8. 08

    **China–US – Wang Yi met Marco Rubio:** Wang said the two countries should implement the direction agreed by their presidents, manage differences and address China’s legitimate concerns, while reiterating the one-China principle. The meeting provides the latest official signal on bilateral relations following recent negative US statements. [Eastmoney]

  9. 09

    **Central SOEs – reform and technology push:** SASAC’s central-enterprise leaders’ seminar called for new reorganised and newly established SOEs to accelerate operations, alongside greater original innovation and core-technology research. Central SOEs generated RMB1.4tn of aggregate profit in the first half. [Eastmoney]

  10. 10

    **Sports industry – first national five-year sector plan:** The General Administration of Sport released the Sports Power Construction 15th Five-Year Plan, targeting a material improvement in overall development quality by 2030. It covers mass fitness, competitive sports, youth programmes and expansion of the sports industry, relevant for sporting goods, venues, tourism and lottery operators. [Eastmoney]

  11. 11

    **Shanghai marine economy – offshore equipment prioritised:** Shanghai’s marine-development plan calls for large-megawatt offshore wind deployment and further development of marine sensors, intelligent robots and submersibles. The plan supports offshore renewables and specialised-equipment suppliers. [Eastmoney]

  12. 12

    **Jereh – US$1.465bn data-centre power order:** Jereh Group (002353 CH) said its controlled subsidiary signed a gas-turbine generator supply contract with an international cloud provider worth about RMB9.95bn, equivalent to 61.33% of 2025 revenue. Deliveries are scheduled in batches by November 2027. [Eastmoney]

  13. 13

    **China power-equipment exporters – major contracts:** PowerChina (601669 CH) signed an approximately RMB8.93bn subcontract for the water-transfer system of Iraq’s Basra desalination project. Separately, Xuji Electric and China XD disclosed State Grid awards worth RMB1.25bn and RMB4.13bn respectively, reinforcing the grid-equipment order pipeline. [Eastmoney]

  14. 14

    **Biwin Storage – shares to be bought and cancelled:** Biwin Storage (688525 CH) chairman Sun Chengsi proposed a RMB200m–250m repurchase, with the shares to be cancelled and registered capital reduced. The proposed ceiling is RMB558.44 per share. [Eastmoney]

  15. 15

    **Xiaomi – HK buyback continued:** Xiaomi (1810 HK) spent HK$49.995m repurchasing 1.875m shares on Wednesday at HK$26.60–26.76 each. The post-close disclosure extends the company’s active 2026 capital-return programme. [Zhitong]

  16. 16

    **Orbbec – planning an H-share listing:** Orbbec (688322 CH), a 3D-vision and robotics-sensing supplier, said it is preparing to issue H shares and list in Hong Kong. The offer structure and timetable remain under discussion, but the filing adds another technology name to the A-to-H pipeline. [Eastmoney]

  17. 17

    **Luxshare Precision – RMB1bn repurchase completed to date:** Luxshare (002475 CH) said it had bought 17.67m shares, or 0.24% of capital, for approximately RMB1bn as of July 22. The shares are intended for employee ownership or equity incentives. [Sina]

  18. 18

    **Rongbai Technology – battery materials returned to profit:** Rongbai (688005 CH) reported H1 revenue up 39.6% to RMB8.72bn and net profit of RMB109m versus a prior-year loss. Overseas shipments increased, lithium-manganese-iron-phosphate sales rose about 50%, and sodium-ion cathode volumes expanded sharply. [Eastmoney]
