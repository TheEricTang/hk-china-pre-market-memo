Intraday Market Memo | 21 Jul 2026 | HK/China Update

(covers 20 Jul 2026 16:00 HKT close → 21 Jul 2026 13:34 HKT research cutoff)

  1. 01

    Intraday tape – technology rebounds: At the midday break, the Hang Seng Index was flat at +0.03%, while the Hang Seng Tech Index gained 1.83%. Onshore, the Shanghai Composite rose 0.62%, Shenzhen gained 3.41% and ChiNext surged 5.20%; semiconductors rallied about 8% after an early sell-off, while oil, coal and financials lagged. [Eastmoney] [Eastmoney]

  2. 02

    Overnight US – optics, storage and ADRs outperform: The Dow fell 0.59%, the S&P 500 lost 0.19% and the Nasdaq slipped 0.05%. Lumentum and Credo gained more than 4%, Marvell rose over 3%, Coherent, SanDisk and Western Digital added more than 2%, and Seagate and Micron gained over 1%; the Nasdaq Golden Dragon Index rose 0.90%, led by Alibaba and Trip.com above 4% and JD.com above 3%. [Eastmoney]

  3. 03

    State Council – service sector and “six networks”: Premier Li Qiang chaired a State Council meeting calling for faster implementation and stronger funding support for water, new power-grid, computing, next-generation communications, urban underground-pipeline and logistics networks. The meeting also urged differentiated support for productive and consumer services and approved the 15th Five-Year Plan for intellectual-property protection and utilisation. [Xinhua]

  4. 04

    Insurers – long-term equity support: PICC, China Life, Ping An, China Pacific Insurance and New China Life issued coordinated statements backing the domestic capital market. The insurers said they would increase or optimise equity exposure, with technology, advanced manufacturing, infrastructure, consumption and new energy among the identified areas. [Eastmoney]

  5. 05

    PBOC liquidity: The central bank conducted RMB253bn of seven-day reverse repos at an unchanged 1.40% rate. The operation provides short-term funding support as equities attempt to stabilise following recent technology-sector volatility. [Eastmoney]

  6. 06

    Transport policy – rail projects and new-energy trucks: Officials said the railway sector would proceed with major projects during the 15th Five-Year Plan while maintaining an “appropriately advanced, but not excessively advanced” construction pace. The government will also continue commercial-truck scrappage and replacement support, prioritising new-energy heavy trucks; relevant names include CRRC, railway contractors, commercial-vehicle makers and battery suppliers. [The Paper] [Eastmoney]

  7. 07

    Xiaomi – reported handset target increase: Xiaomi reportedly raised its 2026 smartphone shipment target to 110m units, with most of the incremental volume expected from lower-priced models. The report provides a positive volume read-through for Xiaomi 1810 HK and handset-component suppliers, although the target has not been confirmed through a company announcement. [Eastmoney] [on.cc]

  8. 08

    HKEX – longer trading hours under consideration: Bloomberg reported that Hong Kong Exchanges and Clearing is considering starting continuous stock trading at 09:00 HKT, 30 minutes earlier, and eliminating the one-hour lunch break. The reported proposal has been discussed with brokers and trading firms but has not been announced as a final rule. [Eastmoney]

  9. 09

    US–Canada trade – additional auto tariffs: The White House announced an additional 50% tariff on certain Canadian products, citing Canadian measures affecting US automotive and auto-parts trade. The development adds a fresh supply-chain and pricing variable for global automakers, components manufacturers and metals markets. [Eastmoney]

  10. 10

    CATL and SDIC Power – hydropower partnership: SDIC Power subsidiary Yalong Hydro plans to establish a joint venture with CATL 300750 CH to develop the Yagen II hydropower station. The agreement extends CATL’s involvement beyond batteries into large-scale renewable-power infrastructure and storage-linked energy systems. [Eastmoney]

  11. 11

    Hangdian – AI copper-foil capacity: Hangdian 603618 CH proposed a private placement of up to RMB2.88bn for an optical-fibre materials and advanced-fibre manufacturing complex and a 30,000-tonne annual high-end electronic copper-foil project serving AI circuits. The plan is relevant to domestic data-centre, PCB and high-speed interconnect supply chains. [Eastmoney]

  12. 12

    Shareholder support – buybacks and increases: Huayou Cobalt proposed a RMB600m–1bn buyback, Sany Heavy Industry’s chairman proposed RMB400m–800m, and China State Construction’s parent plans to purchase RMB500m–1bn of A-shares. Shanghai Bank managers, Sichuan Investment Energy’s parent and Huaqin Technology’s chairman also announced purchase plans, including Huaqin H-shares. [Eastmoney]

  13. 13

    AI and cloud investment – listed-company projects: Donghua Software proposed raising up to RMB2.029bn for an intelligent-computing centre and AI applications spanning housing, finance and water networks. Yushun Electronics separately disclosed a five-year RMB731m computing-server leasing contract, adding to the domestic AI-infrastructure order pipeline. [Eastmoney]

  14. 14

    Zirconia – producer announces price increase: Sinocera Materials 300285 CH will raise zirconia-powder prices by approximately 10%–40% from 27 July, citing higher raw-material and auxiliary-input costs. The move is relevant to advanced ceramics, electronics, dental materials and solid-state battery components. [Eastmoney]
