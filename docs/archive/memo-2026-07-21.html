Morning Market Memo | 21 Jul 2026 | HK/China Pre-Open

(covers 20 Jul 2026 16:00 HKT close → 21 Jul 2026 12:15 HKT research cutoff)

  1. 01

    State Council – services and infrastructure: Premier Li Qiang chaired a meeting calling for tailored support to expand services and faster implementation of the water, power, computing, communications, underground-pipeline and logistics “six networks,” backed by funding and operational reforms. The meeting also approved the 15th Five-Year Plan for intellectual-property protection and discussed social-security changes for flexible workers, migrant housing and disability care. ([forestry.gov.cn](https://www.forestry.gov.cn/lyj/1/szxx/20260720/681348.html)) [Xinhua]

  2. 02

    CSRC – market stability and investor returns: In an overnight disclosure, Chairman Wu Qing said the regulator would maintain stable market operation, improve listed-company transparency and authenticity, strengthen investor protection and push companies to deliver better returns. Investor representatives called for countercyclical management of primary and secondary markets, more long-term capital and tighter oversight of quantitative trading and AI use. ([csrc.gov.cn](https://www.csrc.gov.cn/csrc/c100028/c7646232/content.shtml)) [CSRC]

  3. 03

    A-share buyback and buying plans exceed RMB120bn: Nearly 30 companies announced buybacks or shareholder purchases on Monday, with combined upper limits above RMB120bn. Plans included RMB15bn–30bn of buying in China Three Gorges Renewables, RMB10bn–20bn across Chalco’s A and H shares, and buybacks of up to RMB10bn each by Huayou Cobalt and NARI Technology. ([finance.eastmoney.com](https://finance.eastmoney.com/a/202607203813695988.html)) [Beijing Business Today]

  4. 04

    Insurers signal higher equity allocation: China Pacific Insurance, Ping An and New China Life issued statements supporting the domestic capital market; New China Life said it would increase equity-investment intensity and allocate more capital to technology innovation and new productive forces. This adds long-duration institutional support after the recent technology-led correction. ([finance.eastmoney.com](https://finance.eastmoney.com/a/202607203813518365.html)) [Eastmoney]

  5. 05

    HKEX – cash-market extension remains preliminary: HKEX said reports that it may extend stock trading hours or cancel the lunch break concern proposals still at a very early stage. Its immediate priority is studying longer derivatives-market hours, with implementation dependent on market consultation, regulatory approval and coordination with Stock Connect. ([finance.eastmoney.com](https://finance.eastmoney.com/a/202607203813299030.html)) [Securities Times]

  6. 06

    Citi upgrades China equities: Citi raised China from “tactical neutral” to “overweight” in emerging-market allocation, citing light investor positioning and scope for market gains to broaden beyond the previously concentrated AI and chip trade. It simultaneously cut Korea to tactical neutral, a relative-allocation positive for A shares and Hong Kong. ([thepaper.cn](https://www.thepaper.cn/newsDetail_forward_33624668)) [The Paper]

  7. 07

    China–US leadership contact: The Foreign Ministry said China and the US remain in communication over leader-level interactions this year, but did not confirm reports of a September US visit by President Xi Jinping. Foreign Minister Wang Yi will attend China–ASEAN meetings on 22 July and the SCO foreign ministers’ meeting on 23–24 July. ([mfa.gov.cn](https://www.mfa.gov.cn/web/wjdt_674879/fyrbt_674889/202607/t20260720_11986571.shtml?utm_source=openai)) [MFA]

  8. 08

    Zirconia – Guoci Materials raises prices: Guoci Materials (300285 CH) will increase zirconia-powder prices by approximately 10%–40% from 27 July because of higher raw-material and auxiliary-material costs. The announcement provides a fresh pricing catalyst for zirconia ceramics and related advanced-material suppliers. ([finance.eastmoney.com](https://finance.eastmoney.com/a/202607203813518365.html)) [Eastmoney]

  9. 09

    Hangzhou Cable – RMB28.8bn AI-material expansion: Hangzhou Cable (603618 CH) proposed a private placement of up to RMB28.8bn, including RMB13.81bn for a fibre-preform, quartz and next-generation fibre factory and RMB14.99bn for annual capacity of 30,000 tonnes of high-end copper foil used in AI circuit boards. The projects target upstream optical-network and AI-server materials. ([finance.eastmoney.com](https://finance.eastmoney.com/a/202607203813619365.html?utm_source=openai)) [Securities Times]

  10. 10

    CATL joins RMB33.4bn hydropower project: SDIC Power’s Yalong River Hydropower unit will form a venture with CATL (300750 CH) to develop the Yagen II hydropower station. The project has planned investment of RMB33.394bn, 2.4GW of capacity and expected annual generation of about 8.65TWh, with the first unit targeted for 2035. ([finance.eastmoney.com](https://finance.eastmoney.com/a/202607213813789522.html?utm_source=openai)) [Shanghai Securities News]

  11. 11

    Yangdian Technology – RMB860m computing contract: Yangdian Technology’s (301012 CH) new computing subsidiary signed a five-year contract worth RMB859.8m with an unnamed intelligent-driving customer, equivalent to 67.2% of the company’s 2025 revenue. The first 32 of up to 128 computing nodes must be delivered by 31 August, leaving execution and funding as key risks. ([finance.eastmoney.com](https://finance.eastmoney.com/a/202607203813655562.html)) [CLS]

  12. 12

    Donghua Software – RMB20.3bn AI placement: Donghua Software (002065 CH) proposed raising up to RMB20.29bn for an intelligent-computing centre, AI-native digital-finance applications, urban housing agents and a digital-twin water-network platform, plus working capital. The plan is another sizeable financing proposal directed at domestic computing infrastructure and industry-specific AI. ([finance.eastmoney.com](https://finance.eastmoney.com/a/202607203813518365.html)) [Eastmoney]

  13. 13

    Zhimianda – three-year order restriction: Zhimianda Electronics (688636 CH) said a special matter will restrict its ability to obtain orders in the specialised sector for three years, with an expected negative impact on operations and finances. Existing signed contracts are expected to remain unaffected, while the company will seek customers in other embedded-computing markets. ([finance.eastmoney.com](https://finance.eastmoney.com/a/202607203813518365.html)) [Eastmoney]

  14. 14

    BOE begins A-share cancellation buyback: BOE Technology (000725 CH) bought 8.49m A shares for RMB49.9m on 20 July at RMB5.84–5.90 each, its first A-share purchase under the cancellation programme. It has separately accumulated 85.04m B shares for HKD393.4m, equal to 12.27% of the B-share class. ([paper.cnstock.com](https://paper.cnstock.com/html/2026-07/21/content_2246767.htm)) [Shanghai Securities News]

  15. 15

    Southbound flows – internet names bought: Mainland investors recorded net purchases of HKD1.86bn in Alibaba (9988 HK), HKD1.41bn in NetEase (9999 HK) and HKD472m in Meituan (3690 HK). Tracker Fund, Hang Seng Tech ETF and Tencent (700 HK) saw net selling of HKD5.19bn, HKD1.28bn and HKD580m respectively. ([m.jrj.com.cn](https://m.jrj.com.cn/madapter/finance/2026/07/20183557844134.shtml?utm_source=openai)) [JRJ]

  16. 16

    Overnight US markets – optics, storage and China ADRs outperform: The Dow fell 0.59%, S&P 500 lost 0.19% and Nasdaq eased 0.05%. Lumentum and Credo rose more than 4%, Marvell over 3%, and SanDisk, Western Digital and Coherent over 2%; the Nasdaq Golden Dragon index gained nearly 1%, with Alibaba up 4.69%, supporting HK optical, storage and internet peers. ([finance.eastmoney.com](https://finance.eastmoney.com/a/202607203813630286.html)) [Eastmoney]

  17. 17

    Cross-assets – oil and yields firm: Brent rose about 1.3% to US$89.22 as US–Iran tensions continued; Eastmoney’s WTI continuous contract gained 0.78% to US$82.42. The US 10-year yield rose 4.4bp to 4.592%, DXY added 0.19% to 100.957, COMEX gold slipped 0.17% to US$4,011.8 and silver gained 0.60% to US$56.665, relevant to Chinese oil producers, airlines and precious-metals names. ([finance.sina.com.cn](https://finance.sina.com.cn/stock/usstock/c/2026-07-21/doc-iniipatu4864105.shtml)) [Eastmoney]
