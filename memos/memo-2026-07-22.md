Morning Market Memo | 22 Jul 2026 | HK/China Pre-Open
(covers 21 Jul 2026 16:00 HKT close → 22 Jul 2026 07:55 HKT research cutoff)

- **Capital-market stability:** The CSRC said its leadership met 29 representatives from listed companies, financial institutions and academia on 20–21 July. Proposals covered long-term-capital assessment, quantitative-trading rules, corporate governance and institutionalising market-stabilisation mechanisms; the regulator pledged stronger market foundations and investor protection. [[CSRC](https://www.csrc.gov.cn/csrc/c100028/c7646684/content.shtml)] [[Eastmoney](https://finance.eastmoney.com/a/202607223816086570.html)]

- **IPv6 rollout:** The Central Cyberspace Affairs Commission issued its 2026–30 IPv6 implementation plan after Tuesday’s close. By 2027, China targets 900 million active IPv6 users and a 38% share of internet traffic, supporting telecom equipment, cloud infrastructure, network-security and data-centre suppliers. [[Jiangsu Cyberspace](https://www.jswx.gov.cn/zhengce/fagui/202607/t20260721_1345845.shtml)]

- **Zhongji Innolight Hong Kong listing:** Zhongji Innolight (300308 CH) plans to issue 54.5 million H shares, subject to any over-allotment, with an indicated maximum offer price of HK$1,010 per share. Pricing is scheduled for 28 July and Hong Kong trading for 30 July, adding a major optical-module listing to the local AI-hardware complex. [[Eastmoney](https://finance.eastmoney.com/a/202607223816113696.html)]

- **GigaDevice funds DRAM expansion:** GigaDevice (603986 CH/3986 HK) approved a RMB500 million capital injection into wholly owned Zhuhai Xincun Semiconductor using A-share proceeds. The funding supports its DRAM programme, whose product scope includes DDR3, DDR4, LPDDR4 and LPDDR5, reinforcing domestic-memory exposure after the overnight rally in global storage stocks. [[Eastmoney](https://finance.eastmoney.com/a/202607213815919619.html)]

- **Yuanjie Technology profit surge:** Optical-chip maker Yuanjie Technology (688498 CH) guided first-half revenue to RMB900–950 million, up 339%–364%, and net profit to RMB600–650 million, up 1,197%–1,305%. Data-centre sales and higher gross margins were the main drivers, although investment fair-value gains also contributed; the announcement is relevant to the wider optical-component chain. [[Eastmoney](https://finance.eastmoney.com/a/202607213816046854.html)]

- **A-share buyback wave:** Sungrow (300274 CH) proposed a RMB500 million–RMB1 billion repurchase, while Jiuli Special Materials outlined RMB200–400 million and Sanyou Medical RMB90–180 million programmes. UBTech-controlled Fenglong also received a proposal for a RMB30–60 million repurchase, extending post-close support across renewable energy, medical devices and robotics. [[Shanghai Securities News](https://finance.eastmoney.com/a/202607213816047048.html)] [[Eastmoney](https://finance.eastmoney.com/a/202607213816020548.html)]

- **Vanke receives further shareholder funding:** Shenzhen Metro will provide Vanke (000002 CH/2202 HK) with up to RMB519 million to repay publicly issued bond principal, interest and designated borrowing costs. The loan runs for no more than three years at one-year LPR minus 71 basis points, currently 2.29%, providing additional near-term liquidity support. [[Yicai](https://www.yicai.com/news/103286409.html)]

- **Nvidia details Vera CPU:** Nvidia disclosed additional specifications for its Vera data-centre CPU and said chips were delivered in June to customers including OpenAI, Anthropic and SpaceX. Vera forms part of the Rubin AI-server platform now being adopted by major cloud providers, keeping attention on Chinese server, PCB, cooling and optical-interconnect suppliers. [[Eastmoney](https://finance.eastmoney.com/a/202607223816082260.html)]

- **New US tariffs signalled:** US Trade Representative Jamieson Greer said Washington would soon unveil a replacement for the expiring 10% global import tariff. The administration expects to use Section 301 to impose tariffs of 10%–12.5% on 60 countries and regions over alleged forced-labour concerns, although the announcement date remains unspecified; exporters, electronics and shipping names are exposed. [[CCTV](https://finance.eastmoney.com/a/202607223816100518.html)]

- **Overnight US markets:** The Dow rose 0.74%, S&P 500 gained 0.89% and Nasdaq advanced 1.29%. Intel and AMD added more than 8%, TSMC over 5% and Nvidia nearly 2%; SanDisk jumped over 14%, Micron and Western Digital over 12%, Seagate over 11%, while Coherent and Lumentum gained over 11% and 9%. The Nasdaq Golden Dragon Index nevertheless fell 0.68%, with Tencent and NetEase ADRs down over 3% and Alibaba nearly 2%. [[Eastmoney](https://finance.eastmoney.com/a/202607223816097837.html)]

- **Cross-assets:** WTI rose 2.50% to US$84.54 a barrel; COMEX gold gained 1.65% to US$4,082.20 an ounce and silver advanced 3.50% to US$59.07. The dollar index added 0.21% to 101.172, while the US 10-year yield rose 3.44 basis points to 4.6261% and the two-year increased 5.51 basis points to 4.2573%. [[Eastmoney](https://finance.eastmoney.com/a/202607213816020548.html)]

- **Chile storm disrupts copper operations:** Severe winter weather restricted activity at mines and ports used by Codelco, Antofagasta and Anglo American, prompting safety measures and partial operational adjustments. LME copper closed US$263 higher at US$13,885 a tonne, providing an overnight read-through for Chinese copper miners, smelters and cable producers.
