Morning Market Memo | 29 Jun 2026 | HK/China Pre-Open

Overnight setup: a memory-led semis rout dragged the Nasdaq to a 5th straight down day and crushed Asian tech Friday, so HK/A-share AI-hardware and storage names open under pressure — even as onshore policy flow stays supportive.

- Global semis rout leads the tape: Friday's US session saw the Nasdaq fall a 5th straight day (‑0.24% to 25,298; week ‑4.6%), Dow ‑0.09%, S&P ‑0.05%, with the Philadelphia Semiconductor Index ‑5.29%. onsemi fell 24% (worst since 2020), Western Digital ‑13%, Seagate ‑12%, SanDisk ‑10%, NXP and Qualcomm ‑7%, Micron ‑6%, Nvidia ‑1.6%; the trigger was a report OpenAI may delay its IPO, reviving AI-capex sustainability worries. Asia was hit harder — SoftBank ‑12%, Korea's KOSPI ‑5.81% to 8,411. Read-through to HK/A hardware (SMIC 0981 HK, Hua Hong 1347 HK) and optical-module/PCB names. Samsung is set to unveil a KRW 1,000tn (~US$651bn) decade-long plan and SK Hynix a US$29bn Nasdaq raise. Positive for A-share storage, though the market is now trading cost-squeeze on downstream margins. [[Sina](https://finance.sina.com.cn/stock/usstock/c/2026-06-28/doc-iniewssk1096414.shtml)]
