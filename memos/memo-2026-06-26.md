Morning Market Memo | 26 Jun 2026 | HK/China Pre-Open

Dominant overnight event: Micron's blowout confirmed the memory/storage super-cycle, lifting US chip names to record closes and validating the A-share storage rally — but megacaps (Apple, China ADRs) fell, so the read-through is selective.

- **Storage super-cycle – Micron's "王炸" print:** AI-memory leader Micron posted FQ3 (Mar–May) revenue up 346% YoY to US$41.46bn, gross margin 84.9% and adjusted EPS US$25.11 (vs ~$20.28 est), and guided FQ4 revenue to ~US$50bn. It has locked ~US$22bn of long-term customer commitments and said HBM4 12-high is ramping twice as fast as HBM3E (>US$1bn HBM4 shipped); CEO Mehrotra sees shortages persisting into 2028. Shares rose up to 16% after-hours. Direct read-through to HK/China memory and storage-chain names. [[Eastmoney](https://finance.eastmoney.com/a/202606253781873757.html)]

Storage-chip names exploded — Ingenic (300223) +20% limit, Demingli limit-up; advanced packaging (JCET) and CPO/optical also led, with RMB11.5bn net inflow to components. Micron's print is a fresh catalyst at the open. [[Eastmoney](https://fund.eastmoney.com/a/202606253782992778.html)]

Apple closed -6%+ (biggest drop since Apr 2025), Microsoft -3%+. Negative for Apple-chain assemblers' margins, but reinforces pricing power for memory, MLCC and PCB/CCL suppliers. - **PBOC adds overnight reverse repo for month-end:** The PBOC said 25 Jun it will add an overnight reverse-repo instrument in open-market operations on 29–30 Jun (fixed rate, quantity tender), implementing Gov. Pan Gongsheng's 17 Jun Lujiazui pledge to enrich the toolbox and narrow the temporary repo band to the 7-day rate ±25bp. Brokers say it fills a maturity gap and gives more precise quarter-end liquidity control, with overnight rates seen converging to 1.30–1.40% and a possible longer-term shift to an overnight-rate-led framework. Supportive for banks/funding into quarter-end. [[Eastmoney](https://finance.eastmoney.com/a/202606253782746219.html)]

Separately, China on 22 Jun added 10 US entities (incl. Avioxx) to its export-control list and barred government procurement from 46 US firms, retaliating against the US "Chinese military companies" listing. Constructive tone on tariffs, but the friction track continues. [[Eastmoney](https://finance.eastmoney.com/a/202606253782997368.html)]

Positive for grid, renewables, nuclear, storage and lithium/silicon names. [[Eastmoney](https://finance.eastmoney.com/a/202606263783486691.html)]
